Fueling for Tour de France: How Champions Eat to Win
Learn how elite cyclists fuel for the Tour de France, plus research on carbohydrate periodization and how custom nutrition plans enhance performance.
Ever wondered how the champions of the Tour de France fuel their rides to victory? Discover the hidden nutrition secrets that powered four Tour de France wins with Professor
James Morton
, the Hexis team, and special guest
Bob Jungels
, former Tour de France rider. They will unveil insider strategies with real-world insights from Bob Jungels to transform your performance and fuel like a pro.
Webinar Guests:
Professor James Morton (Program Director INEOS X & former Head of Nutrition for Team Sky)
Bob Jungels (former Red Bull BORA – hansgrohe rider)
Dr. David Dunne (Hexis CEO & Co-Founder)
Dr. Sam Impey (Hexis Co-Founder & former Head of Nutrition at British Cycling)
The Evolution of Carbohydrates in Cycling:
“I think what’s interesting during the last years, the amounts of carbohydrates that we eat during the exercise before and after, has increased so much, and I think it just, you know, the capacity or the performance on the bike, and the recovery, the recovery has just improved so much.” — Cyclist Bob Jungles
The Power of Carbs in Exercise:
“Consuming high carbohydrate intake for several days increases muscle glycogen stores and then improves exercise capacity. And when you look at the exercise capacity data, you can see the carbohydrate is making people ride for longer to the extent of 2 to 3 hours longer.” — Professor James Morton
“Really fundamentally, every athlete is unique, and we should all have unique fueling plans.”
Dr. Sam Impey
Race Nutrition:
“During the race, we rarely go below 80 grams an hour. And as soon as the race starts to get harder, towards the end or even from the middle, almost every stage nowadays is at least 120 grams.” — Bob Jungles
The Variability in Fuel Requirements:
“I really hope that shows how just how much variability you can have day to day and why it’s important to use all that data that’s available to make the bespoke recommendations to enable each athlete to perform optimally.” — Dr. Sam Impey
