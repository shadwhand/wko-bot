Dreaming is the first spark of action and pathway to a plan. What does your goal look like this year?
What does it mean to dream? Does it entail abstract wishes and longing for impossible realities? Is it a solitary act, done alone or only in one’s own head? While this may be the case when you lay your head against your pillow at night, dreaming is really the first spark of action toward a goal. It is the pathway to a plan, and the start of an exciting journey toward a rewarding accomplishment.
What does your goal look like this year?
Whether you have a podium spot in mind, a specific time for the clock to read when you cross that finish line, or you just want to try something new—one of the most rewarding things about being an endurance athlete is the clean slate you get each year to set new performance expectations for yourself.
At TrainingPeaks, we believe that each year during this time, we should all seize the opportunity to dream up new goals so that our upcoming season planning can begin as soon as possible. The key is not only to set a particular performance goal, but also to piece together a strong framework of training, analyzing and accountability. This holds true whether you’re
training yourself
, following a
training plan
, or working with an
expert coach.
