# TMT #74: FTP Training Mistakes (And Solutions)

**Date:** March 14, 2026 | **Duration:** 1:11 | **Hosts:** Kolie Moore, Erica, Fabiano

Three of Empirical Cycling's most experienced coaches (~75 years collective coaching experience, 1,000+ individual seasons coached) discuss common mistakes when training and building FTP -- testing errors, interval execution, progression pitfalls, and adaptation timelines.

---

## Key Claims

### Testing Mistakes

- **The best FTP test is riding at FTP** -- feel it out by starting conservative, building through the effort; the best predictor of performance is performance itself [E] (TMT-74, Kolie Moore)
- **Extrapolating FTP from sub-FTP intervals is unreliable** -- completing 3x20 sweet spot does not tell you what you can hold for 30-40 minutes at FTP; 90 minutes at sweet spot does not necessarily mean FTP has risen [E] (TMT-74, Kolie Moore)
- **The 2x8 minute test overestimates FTP by massive margins** -- historical protocol (average two 8-min efforts, take 90%); Kolie tested at 330W with this method when his actual FTP was ~270W. The 8-minute duration allows too much anaerobic contribution [E] (TMT-74, Kolie Moore, Erica)
- **Lactate tests are oversold and often misleading** -- 4 mmol is a population average (ranges 3.8-4.2); the individual is not the average (fallacy of division). Lactate can both drastically overestimate and underestimate FTP [E] (TMT-74, Kolie Moore)
  - Example: a world champion track cyclist's lactate-derived FTP was 30-40W below his actual threshold, verified by 40-50 min sustained efforts [E]
  - >10 different methodologies exist for lactate threshold determination -- "if there are too many ways to do it, probably none of them work as it should" [E] (TMT-74, Fabiano)
- **CPX (cardiopulmonary exercise testing) tends to overestimate thresholds** -- looks good on paper but athletes fail 2x20 at the prescribed LT2 values [E] (TMT-74, Fabiano)
- **Fixed FTP definitions introduce systematic errors** -- dividing 5-min power by 1.20 will massively overestimate FTP for sprinter phenotypes; 95% of 20-min test can be off by 5% (Kolie at 90%, not 95%). These percentage-based estimates are population averages [E] (TMT-74, Kolie Moore)
- **Pre-decided FTP numbers bias execution** -- athletes who "want this to be my number" ride too hard on early intervals and then fade, losing the training stimulus. Approach with an open mind; let RPE calibrate the target [E] (TMT-74, Erica)

### Interval Execution Mistakes

- **Insufficient rest before threshold sessions degrades RPE calibration** -- physical fatigue and mental/life stress shift RPE scale, making threshold feel harder than it should [E] (TMT-74, Erica)
- **Short threshold intervals risk drifting into VO2max intensity** -- especially for beginners or winter indoor training; start at 12+ minutes minimum and build longer. Overly short intervals explain why athletes cannot progress time-in-zone [E] (TMT-74, Fabiano)
- **Start conservative, build through the session** -- first interval should be 5-10W below target; assess legs, then push on later intervals. The last interval can go all-in. Far better than overpacing the first interval and fading [E] (TMT-74, Fabiano, Kolie Moore)
- **Over-unders are effective but full intervals above 105% FTP are suboptimal** -- over-unders (90%/105%) with bursts are fine; sustained 20 minutes over 105% is not threshold training [E] (TMT-74, Kolie Moore)
- **Outdoor interval stochasticity does not invalidate the workout** -- average and normalized power within 1W is a great interval, even if the power trace is not erg-mode smooth. WKO5's rule-based auto-detection of intervals is a good proxy for quality [E] (TMT-74, Kolie Moore, Fabiano)

### Environmental & Fueling Mistakes

- **Indoor heat buildup is a major limiter** -- 65-75F indoor temps mean overheating; fans + ice water help but tempo/sweet spot may be the realistic ceiling indoors for many athletes. FTP indoors may need a 10-15W reduction [E] (TMT-74, Kolie Moore)
- **Under-fueling threshold sessions sabotages adaptation** -- 2x20 at FTP is a massive kJ day requiring carbs on the bike. Missing post-workout nutrition compounds into the next session (Fabiano's athlete example: failed Monday after Sunday under-fueling) [E] (TMT-74, Fabiano)
- **Morning sessions require deliberate pre-hydration and fueling** -- 8 hours of sleep means dehydration and glycogen depletion; gel at the start or drink mix is minimum. Hydration may be as big an issue as fueling for early-morning riders [E] (TMT-74, Kolie Moore, Erica)
- **Indoor-to-outdoor transition requires RPE recalibration** -- physics, thermoregulation, and terrain all change; athletes must explicitly recalibrate their threshold perception when moving outside in spring [E] (TMT-74, Fabiano)
- **Terrain matters for FTP expression** -- some athletes show 10-20W difference between flat and climbing. Feedback trainers (like rollers) feel like climbing; flywheel trainers (like Tacx Neo) feel like flat ground. Train at the terrain that matches your race demands [E] (TMT-74, Kolie Moore)
- **Shifting skill affects interval quality** -- even experienced racers accelerate from 40-50 RPM after rest intervals. Deliberate practice of big/small ring shifting between intervals keeps cadence in the power band [E] (TMT-74, Kolie Moore)

### Progression & Plateau Mistakes

- **FTP intervals eventually stop raising FTP** -- early-season threshold work raises FTP, but there is a point when it only extends TTE, not the watt number itself. Extrapolating early-season gains ad infinitum leads to over-training [E] (TMT-74, Kolie Moore)
- **Raising power by 3-5W per week without validation** -- assumes linear FTP growth that does not exist; eventually rides are above threshold without the athlete realizing it [E] (TMT-74, Kolie Moore)
- **The VO2max ceiling applies** -- "the house analogy: VO2 is the roof, FTP is the ceiling." When FTP work stops working, the roof may need raising. This explains plateaus that do not respond to more threshold training [E] (TMT-74, Fabiano)
- **Building FTP through race season is usually counterproductive** -- unpredictable race fatigue (e.g. 100km in a breakaway) disrupts recovery timing. In-season, accept stable FTP and manage fatigue [E] (TMT-74, Kolie Moore)
- **FTP does not change much day-to-day** -- perceived FTP drops mid-week are almost always fatigue, heat, or nutrition masking true capacity, not actual fitness loss. Use FTP as a fatigue buoy [E] (TMT-74, Kolie Moore)
- **After a big FTP bump, RPE feels disjointed** -- new higher FTP feels harder than expected because of previous RPE rut. Athletes need to trust the numbers and train through the discomfort. TTE may also temporarily drop from 60 to 35 min after a bump, requiring rebuild [E] (TMT-74, Kolie Moore)

### Training Variety & Mental Engagement

- **Over-indexing on FTP training has opportunity costs** -- Kolie has given less FTP-specific training over the past 2-3 years without any loss of FTP, TTE, or fitness gains. Variety of efforts is better for overall fitness and mental engagement [E] (TMT-74, Kolie Moore)
- **Block training is overused because it is simple** -- athletes gravitate toward single-focus blocks because they are easy to follow, but variety within the week is often more effective and more sustainable [E] (TMT-74, Kolie Moore)
- **Sweet spot and threshold are interchangeable in practice** -- if sweet spot intervals get very long (time-inefficient), move to threshold. If threshold is impractical (indoors/heat), sweet spot is fine. "Everything's a test" during a threshold block [E] (TMT-74, Kolie Moore, Erica)
- **Mental engagement during intervals matters** -- focus on power output, breathing, body sensations. Do not zone out. Being present during intervals provides both better execution and more useful feedback to your coach [E] (TMT-74, Erica)

### Listener Q&A Highlights

- **Extensive vs intensive FTP intervals:** Kolie prefers thinking in terms of TTE extension vs VO2max (raising the roof), not the WKO5 "FTP intensive" category. FTP requires both muscular metabolic fitness AND aerobic capacity -- it is an "and," not an "or" [E] (TMT-74)
- **Staying under FTP for TTE training:** Yes, riding slightly under FTP (with over-unders) is optimal. The classic "92-105% FTP" threshold zone leads people to ride too hard; over-unders are better than sustained 105% [E] (TMT-74)
- **Signals to move from sweet spot to threshold to VO2max:** Sweet spot-to-threshold is interchangeable; move when sweet spot intervals become impractically long. Threshold-to-VO2max: when FTP plateaus. No separate test needed -- if you're doing threshold, every session is a test [E] (TMT-74)
- **FTP by feel when poorly trained:** Narrow power range (FTP close to max) makes the "feel" signal weak; this improves as fitness develops. Also check nutrition and morning-session hydration [E] (TMT-74)
- **Long intervals getting boring:** Probably time for VO2max work. Over-indexing on any one type of training has opportunity costs. Variety matters for both adaptation and mental health [E] (TMT-74)

## Relevance to Platform

- **`ftp_test.py`**: Add guidance for FTP-by-feel protocol: "start 5-10W conservative, build through effort." Flag when athletes use short-duration tests (8-min, ramp) as primary FTP source. Warn on lactate-test-derived FTP that diverges >10W from rolling FTP.
- **`blocks.py`**: Enforce 12-min minimum threshold interval duration. Template start-conservative progression (5-10W below target for interval 1). Add "FTP as fatigue buoy" logic: if mid-block threshold session drops >15W from recent baseline, flag as fatigue, not fitness loss.
- **`zones.py`**: Support indoor/outdoor FTP split (10-15W indoor reduction if thermal conditions poor). Flag when threshold zone (92-105%) is used to justify sustained 105%+ efforts.
- **`training_load.py`**: Track TTE trajectory after FTP bumps (expect temporary drop). Detect "linear FTP increase assumption" pattern where prescribed watts rise weekly without validation.
- **`nutrition.py`**: Flag high-kJ threshold sessions without on-bike fueling. Alert for back-to-back hard sessions when post-workout refueling was missed.
- **`clinical.py`**: Indoor temperature alert system. Spring transition recalibration reminder when first outdoor rides appear after prolonged indoor block.
- **`ride.py`**: Validate outdoor intervals by average/NP proximity rather than requiring smooth power traces. Use WKO5-style rule-based auto-detection as quality proxy.

## Conflicts with Conventional Wisdom

| Conventional Wisdom | EC TMT-74 Position | Evidence Level |
|--------------------|--------------------|----------------|
| "Lactate testing is the gold standard for FTP" | Oversold; multiple methodologies give different results; 4 mmol is a population average, not individual. Performance-based testing (ride at FTP) is simpler, cheaper, and often more accurate | [E] |
| "CPX/VO2max testing gives precise thresholds" | Tends to overestimate; athletes fail intervals at prescribed values. Functional tests are more reliable for training zones | [E] |
| "95% of 20-min test = FTP for everyone" | Individual ratio ranges from ~90% to ~98%. Sprinter phenotypes are most affected (Kolie at 90%) | [E] |
| "You should aim to raise FTP power every week" | FTP does not increase linearly. Blind weekly power increases lead to riding above threshold without realizing it | [E] |
| "Erg-mode-smooth intervals are better" | Outdoor stochasticity is fine; avg/NP matching target is what matters. Erg mode hides readiness information | [E] |
| "FTP should increase throughout race season" | Usually counterproductive; race fatigue disrupts recovery. Accept stable FTP in-season, manage fatigue | [E] |
| "Block training is the best approach" | Overused because simple; variety within the week often outperforms single-focus blocks for FTP | [E] |
| "Metabolomics will revolutionize training zones" | May eventually, but currently 4 tests/year at $250/test is impractical and unvalidated for training prescription | [O] |
