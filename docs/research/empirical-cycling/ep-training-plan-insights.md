# Empirical Cycling Podcast -- Key Insights for Power Analysis Platform (Batch 2)

## TMT #44: What's So Sweet About Sweetspot

### Key Claims

- **Sweet spot is approximately 90% of FTP** -- sits between tempo and threshold (consensus definition)
- **Sweet spot provides nearly identical adaptations to threshold work** (experience-based + physiological reasoning)
  - Mitochondrial density, lactate clearance, capillarization
  - The difference is cost-benefit ratio (time, fatigue, logistics), not adaptation type
- **Time to Exhaustion (TTE) is the key equalizer between intensities** (experience-based, cornerstone claim)
  - A 2x20 at FTP is NOT equivalent to 2x20 at sweet spot
  - For equivalent stimulus, sweet spot intervals must be pushed to near-exhaustion at that specific intensity
  - Sweet spot TTE: untrained 40-60 min, well-trained 1.5-2 hr, elite 3+ hr
- **Sweet spot is a tool, not a training philosophy** (experience-based)
  - Neither overrated nor underrated: "it's a rated tool"
  - Must integrate with easier endurance work and higher-intensity efforts
- **Sweet spot becomes time-inefficient as fitness improves** (experience-based)
  - Well-trained athletes need 2+ hour sessions for equivalent stimulus to shorter threshold work
  - Time-crunched athletes (<8 hr/week) should use higher intensities on weekdays
- **Sweet spot is excellent when fatigued** -- good mid-week option between races; good "third hard day" in demanding blocks (experience-based)
- **Overestimated FTP makes sweet spot accidentally productive** -- if FTP from ramp test is inflated, "sweet spot" may actually be threshold training (experience-based)
- **No formula to predict sweet spot TTE from FTP TTE** (experience-based)
  - Relationship depends on training age, fiber type, nutrition, individual variation
  - Fallacy of division: average population ratios do not apply to individuals

### Actionable Guidelines

| Guideline | Detail | Module |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Sweet spot target | ~90% FTP | zones |
| Sweet spot TTE ranges | Untrained: 40-60 min; trained: 90-120 min; elite: 180+ min | zones, gap_analysis |
| Time-crunched rule | <8 hr/week: prefer threshold/VO2 on weekdays, sweet spot on weekend long rides | blocks |
| Over-under protocol | 105% / 90% FTP alternating | blocks |
| Best use cases | Ultra-endurance, hilly gran fondos, mid-season between races | blocks |
| Poor use cases | Crit racers (need VO2/anaerobic), time-crunched weekday training | blocks |
| Fueling for sweet spot | Eat heavily day BEFORE; fuel 80-90g carbs/hr on bike | clinical |
| Progressive overload | Track TTE at sweet spot over weeks; if stagnating, move to threshold | training_load |

### Platform Relevance

- **zones**: Define sweet spot band (~88-93% FTP); track TTE progression at this intensity
- **blocks**: Sweet spot block templates with progressive TTE extension; flag when TTE stagnates
- **gap_analysis**: Event-specific recommendation (ultra = extend sweet spot TTE; crit = skip, prioritize VO2)
- **training_load**: Sweet spot TTE as a fitness marker independent of FTP

## TMT #45: The Simplest Training Plan Thought Experiment

### Key Claims

- **A minimal effective plan needs: progressive overload, broad applicability, multi-aspect fitness coverage** (experience-based)
  - Stock plans get 80% of the way; the last 20% (individualization) is where coaching matters
- **The simplest plan: 2 hard days + fill remaining time with endurance** (experience-based, both hosts independently converge)
  - Kolie's plan: 1 threshold day (progressive 4x10 -> 3x15 -> 2x20 -> 1x40+) + 1 VO2max day + endurance fill
  - Rory's plan: virtually identical
- **Threshold intervals should progress in duration, not power** for most athletes (experience-based)
  - Power will naturally rise as TTE extends; forcing power up leads to failed sessions
- **VO2max training is essential even in a minimal plan** -- cannot neglect the aerobic ceiling (experience-based)
  - Minimum dose: 1 session per week
- **Endurance volume is the "fill" -- not optional, not junk** (experience-based)
  - Ride as much as you sustainably can at easy intensity; volume drives durability and aerobic base
- **Sprint training has low opportunity cost** -- a few 10-15 sec sprints at start of endurance rides costs almost nothing in fatigue (experience-based)
- **Rest weeks every 3-4 weeks** even in minimal plans (experience-based)

### Actionable Guidelines

| Guideline | Detail | Module |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Minimum viable plan | 2 hard days (1 threshold + 1 VO2max) + endurance fill | blocks |
| Threshold progression | Extend duration: 4x10 -> 3x15 -> 2x20 -> 1x40+ min at FTP | blocks |
| VO2max minimum dose | 1 session/week; e.g., 5x4 or 4x5 min at 105-120% FTP | blocks |
| Endurance fill | All remaining ride time at <65% FTP | zones, blocks |
| Sprint add-on | 3-5 x 10-15 sec sprints at start of endurance ride | blocks |
| Progressive overload first | Exhaust duration progression before changing protocol | blocks |

### Platform Relevance

- **blocks**: "Simplest plan" template as a baseline recommendation for new users
- **blocks**: Progressive overload tracking for threshold intervals (duration progression)
- **training_load**: Minimum viable plan compliance tracking (2 hard days + endurance per week)
- **zones**: Validate that endurance fill is actually easy (<65% FTP IF)

## TMT #49: Practical Proxies for Stimulus

### Key Claims

- **Power output is the best proxy for training stimulus** -- but only when compared like-for-like (experience-based)
  - Same protocol, same conditions: power going up over weeks = positive adaptation
  - High-cadence VO2 at 310W is not the same as low-cadence at 350W even though power is lower
- **RPE is a critical proxy** but must be calibrated individually (experience-based)
  - "Can I do it again?" test after an interval: if yes at same power, stimulus was appropriate
  - Interval RPE targets: ~8/10 threshold, ~9-9.5/10 VO2max
- **Power + heart rate + RPE triangulation is the gold standard** for assessing workout quality (research-backed, published peer-reviewed article by Kolie Moore)
- **Heart rate is NOT a good proxy for stimulus in isolation** (experience-based)
  - Varies 5-10 BPM day-to-day; higher cadence raises HR without changing muscular stimulus
  - Not hitting max HR during VO2 intervals does NOT mean poor stimulus
  - Useful for pattern recognition (identify workout type, detect illness)
- **Fatigue and soreness are NOT reliable proxies** (experience-based)
  - Delayed feeding increases soreness independent of workout quality
  - A great workout can leave you energized, not destroyed
- **TSS is a volume proxy, not a stimulus proxy** (experience-based)
  - Trap: time-crunched athletes increase TSS by riding harder everywhere
- **HRV is useful for illness detection and sleep quality, not stimulus assessment** (experience-based)
  - Once fit, HRV fluctuations track sleep/illness more than adaptation
  - HRV measurement is highly noise-prone; sensor quality matters enormously
- **Look at metrics on a 90-day scale, not workout-to-workout** (experience-based)
  - WKO5 defaults to 90-day view for good reason

### Actionable Guidelines

| Guideline | Detail | Module |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Primary stimulus proxy | Power (same protocol, same conditions, over weeks) | training_load |
| "Can I do it again?" test | Post-interval self-check | blocks |
| Triangulation method | Power + HR + RPE together; never one alone | training_load |
| Target RPE: threshold | ~8/10 | zones, blocks |
| Target RPE: VO2max | ~9-9.5/10 | zones, blocks |
| Metrics viewing window | 90 days minimum | training_load |
| HRV use case | Illness early warning + sleep quality only | clinical |
| TSS use case | Volume tracking only; NOT stimulus indicator | training_load |

### Platform Relevance

- **training_load**: Build power + HR + RPE triangulation as core workout quality metric
- **training_load**: 90-day rolling trend views as default; de-emphasize single-workout analysis
- **zones**: RPE targets per zone for workout prescription
- **clinical**: HRV trend analysis for illness detection; flag sharp drops
- **blocks**: "Can I do it again?" as interval adequacy check

## TMT #52: Intermediate Training Mistakes (And Solutions)

### Key Claims

- **Intermediate phase = 2-5 years structured training; 10-30W annual FTP gains still possible** (experience-based)
  - Progress becomes non-linear: stagnation followed by breakthroughs
- **Pacing is still a fundamental skill gap for most intermediates** (experience-based)
  - RPE calibration needs constant refinement
- **Progressive overload is forgotten at intermediate level** (experience-based)
  - Plateaued at 2x15? Try 3x15 or 2x20 before seeking exotic protocols
- **Repeating beginner training will NOT produce intermediate gains** (experience-based)
  - Yesterday's potent stimulus is today's maintenance; must introduce new stimuli
- **Over-focus on FTP engine, neglect of race skills** (experience-based)
  - Highest FTP means nothing without pack skills, positioning, sprint timing
- **Aggressive dieting is counterproductive** (experience-based)
  - Losing 1 kg = only a few watts race equivalent; eating MORE often makes you faster
  - LEA symptoms: hormonal disruption, decreased libido, irritability -- in BOTH sexes
- **Recovery is the most under-pulled lever** (experience-based)
  - No ice bath, theragun, or supplement measurably speeds recovery
  - Consistency (just riding regularly) is the single most important factor
- **100% plan compliance is a warning sign**, not a badge of honor -- indicates lack of auto-regulation (experience-based)

### Actionable Guidelines

| Guideline | Detail | Module |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Progressive overload check | Exhaust simple duration/volume progression before any protocol change | blocks |
| Intermediate FTP gain expectation | 10-30W per year | training_load |
| Nutrition priority | Fuel to perform; small sustainable deficit while eating well | clinical |
| LEA warning signs | Hormonal disruption, low libido, irritability, poor focus | clinical |
| Recovery hierarchy | Sleep > nutrition > stress management >> any gadget | clinical |

### Platform Relevance

- **blocks**: Progressive overload tracking with "try longer before trying different" recommendations
- **training_load**: Year-over-year progress tracker with intermediate expectations (10-30W/yr)
- **pacing**: RPE calibration tools; compare intended vs actual pacing
- **clinical**: LEA symptom checklist integration; nutrition adequacy flagging
- **gap_analysis**: Distinguish fitness limiter vs skill limiter vs recovery limiter

## TMT #54: The Truth About Junk Miles

### Key Claims

- **Junk miles = any miles where fatigue cost exceeds adaptive benefit** -- Kolie Moore's unified definition (experience-based)
  - NOT defined by intensity zone; defined by outcome in context
- **Total training volume is a primary driver of endurance adaptation** (experience-based)
  - Commuter effect: athletes lost fitness when commuting stopped (even low intensity)
  - 25hr camp at very low intensity > failing at 20hr camp at moderate intensity
- **"Fatigue security blanket"** -- riding easy days at 70-75% FTP creates insidious chronic fatigue (experience-based)
  - 6-hour ride at 150W is superior to failed 5-hour ride at 200W
- **"All roads lead to PGC-1alpha"** -- tempo, sweet spot, threshold all stimulate mitochondrial biogenesis similarly (research-backed)
  - Zone boundaries are descriptive, not prescriptive
- **"Periodize fun"** -- mental health rides have tangible training value (experience-based)
- **True junk miles are rare**: unsustainable commute stress, unplanned hard efforts on rest days, blind compliance leading to burnout (experience-based)

### Platform Relevance

- **training_load**: Flag rides where fatigue cost is disproportionate to training goal
- **training_load**: Detect "blind compliance" pattern: declining performance + negative sentiment + high load
- **durability**: Volume accumulation giving credit for ALL intensities
- **zones**: Do NOT penalize low-intensity rides as unproductive

## TMT #60: The FTP Training Decision Tree

### Key Claims

- **No single best way to train FTP -- depends on a 3-branch decision tree** (experience-based):
  - Branch 1: Season point (early = extend duration; late = introduce VO2max if plateaued)
  - Branch 2: Training age (novice = duration first; experienced = auto-regulate)
  - Branch 3: Opportunity cost (when FTP plateaus, invest elsewhere)
- **Early season: prioritize adding duration** -- 4x10 -> 3x15 -> 2x20 -> 1x40+ (experience-based)
- **Late season: when FTP plateaus from threshold work, introduce VO2max** (experience-based)
  - Continuing threshold work at plateau only increases TTE, not FTP itself
  - VO2max "raises the roof" for FTP to grow into
- **Sporadic VO2 dosing can work** -- client did 3 sessions month 1, 6 month 2 alongside other training = massive FTP gains (experience-based)
- **Optimal interval structure: 3x20 or 2x30 min at FTP** -- middle ground between fragmented and monolithic (experience-based)
- **Maximum useful TTE: ~60-75 min** -- beyond this, opportunity cost too high; shift to raising FTP itself (experience-based)
- **In-season maintenance: 1 threshold/sweet spot session every 1-2 weeks at RPE 6-7/10** (experience-based)
- **"Freedom of a plateau"** -- accepting stable FTP frees you to become more complete (experience-based)

### Actionable Guidelines

| Guideline | Detail | Module |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| FTP interval progression (early) | 4x10 -> 3x15 -> 2x20 -> 2x25 -> 1x40+ min | blocks |
| FTP plateau signal | FTP unchanged after threshold block + rest + retest | training_load |
| Plateau response | Introduce VO2max block | blocks |
| Optimal interval format | 3x20 or 2x30 min at FTP | blocks |
| Over-under protocol | 90%/105% FTP alternating | blocks |
| Max useful TTE | 60-75 minutes | training_load |
| In-season maintenance | 1 session/1-2 weeks, RPE 6-7/10 | blocks |

### Platform Relevance

- **blocks**: FTP decision tree: detect plateau -> recommend VO2max; detect early season -> recommend duration progression
- **training_load**: TTE tracking at FTP; flag when TTE extends but FTP is flat
- **gap_analysis**: Priority list framework -- rank training focuses by event demands
- **pacing**: Over-under templates with race-specific surge simulation

## Perspectives #38: Training Intensity Distributions, NIRS, and Iliac Arteries (with Jem Arnold)

### Key Claims

- **IPD meta-analysis (~350 athletes, 13 studies): NO single training intensity distribution is statistically superior** (research-backed)
  - Uses 3-zone physiological model (Zone 1 below VT1/LT1, Zone 2 between VT1 and VT2, Zone 3 above VT2)
  - Note: Zone 2 here encompasses what cyclists call tempo, sweet spot, AND threshold
- **Subgroup interaction: competitive athletes slightly favored polarized; recreational slightly favored pyramidal** (research-backed, IPD subgroup)
  - Magnitude: ~1-2% VO2max difference; within day-to-day measurement noise (~5%)
- **~20% of study participants were re-categorized** when analyzing what they ACTUALLY did vs assigned (methodological insight)
  - Individual responses ranged from -20% to +30% around group average
- **Research is incremental; coaching is often ahead of science** (philosophical)
  - Off-season studies overstate intervention effects due to accelerated early gains
  - The longer the time frame, the more interventions converge to similar outcomes
  - "Literally false but metaphorically true" -- things can work for wrong reasons but still be useful
- **Hard start intervals, 30-15 protocols: "super cool but not magic"** (experience-based, Jem Arnold retrospective)
  - All micro-optimizations claim 1-3% improvement; they do NOT compound when stacked
  - Sustainable training template (basics) outperforms session-level optimization long-term
- **FLIA (iliac artery flow limitation/endofibrosis) is underdiagnosed** (research-backed, Jem Arnold PhD)
  - Caused by repetitive hip flexion + anatomical predisposition
  - Symptoms: severe unilateral leg pain/weakness at high intensity ONLY
  - Requires provocative exercise test; NIRS can detect asymmetric deoxygenation between legs
  - If stopped provoking, likely won't progress
- **NIRS thresholds correlate with lactate thresholds at group level but wide individual limits of agreement** (research-backed)

### Actionable Guidelines

| Guideline | Detail | Module |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Intensity distribution | No single "best"; include some high intensity, be consistent, get volume | blocks |
| Competitive athlete lean | Slightly favor polarized | blocks |
| Recreational athlete lean | Slightly favor pyramidal | blocks |
| TID effect magnitude | 1-2% VO2max; within noise for individuals | training_load |
| FLIA screening | Unilateral leg pain at high intensity only; refer for provocative test | clinical |
| Micro-optimization | Pick 1-2 you enjoy; do NOT try to stack all 1-3% claims | blocks |

### Platform Relevance

- **zones**: Support multiple zone models (3-zone physiological + 5-7 zone power); do NOT prescribe one distribution
- **blocks**: Intensity distribution as descriptive metric, not prescriptive target
- **training_load**: Flag dramatic distribution shifts; provide context not judgment
- **clinical**: FLIA screening -- flag persistent unilateral power asymmetry at high intensity
- **gap_analysis**: Model personal response to different training distributions over time

## New Cross-Cutting Themes (from this batch)

### 7. The Sweetspot vs Polarized Debate is Resolved
No single intensity distribution wins. The IPD meta-analysis (Perspectives #38, N=350) found no statistically meaningful overall difference. Individual variation (-20% to +30%) dwarfs any distribution effect (~1-2%). The platform should describe distributions without prescribing one as superior.

### 8. TTE is the Hidden Key Metric
Time to Exhaustion at various intensities (FTP, sweet spot, VO2max) is more actionable than FTP alone. TTE stagnation at FTP signals the need for VO2max work. TTE at sweet spot determines whether sweet spot training is time-efficient for the athlete.

### 9. Progressive Overload Before Protocol Change
Across TMT #44, #45, #52, and #60, the consistent message: exhaust simple duration/volume progression before switching protocols. Most athletes change too often and never fully capture adaptation.

### 10. Proxy Triangulation for Workout Quality
Power alone is insufficient. Power + HR + RPE together (TMT #49) provide the robust picture needed to assess whether training is working. The platform should make this triangulation a first-class feature.
