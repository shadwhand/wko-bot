## 1. Perspectives #36 -- Chronic Underfueling (Traci Carson)

**Guest**: Dr. Traci Carson, epidemiologist specializing in female athlete health (U of Michigan).

### Key Claims

| Claim | Evidence Level | Source |
|-------|---------------|--------|
| Energy Availability (EA) below 30 kcal/kg FFM/day causes significant health consequences (amenorrhea, stress fractures) | **Strong** -- IOC consensus 2023, multiple cohort studies | IOC consensus, Carson dissertation (n=211) |
| EA between 30-45 kcal/kg FFM/day is a gray zone where negative effects begin | **Strong** -- IOC consensus threshold | IOC 2014, 2018, 2023 |
| Athletes can be in chronic LEA without being underweight -- body weight is NOT a reliable indicator | **Strong** -- metabolic adaptation well-documented | Carson, Loucks et al. |
| Large within-day energy deficits trigger stress response even if 24h total intake is adequate | **Strong** -- peer-reviewed paper on calorie distribution and menstrual dysfunction | Referenced paper on nutrient timing |
| Bone damage from LEA in adolescence/young adulthood is largely irreversible | **Strong** -- well-established in bone biology | IOC consensus |
| Overtraining syndrome and RED-S have near-complete overlap in clinical presentation -- "almost just a circle" as a Venn diagram | **Moderate** -- Stellingwerff paper on differential diagnosis | Stellingwerff, Mountjoy, Burke |
| Hormonal contraception masks amenorrhea, preventing early RED-S detection | **Strong** -- well-established clinical observation | IOC consensus |
| One stress fracture strongly predicts subsequent stress fractures at increasing rates | **Strong** -- epidemiological data on runners | Carson's research |

### Actionable Guidelines

- **EA >= 45 kcal/kg FFM/day**: target for optimal function (women; male thresholds not yet established)
- **EA < 30 kcal/kg FFM/day**: RED FLAG -- significant risk of amenorrhea, stress fractures, endocrine disruption
- **Male early warning**: loss of spontaneous morning erections, low libido = suppressed testosterone
- **Female early warning**: irregular menstrual cycles (oligomenorrhea) before full amenorrhea
- **Within-day fueling**: a 1,100 kcal deficit at 9am (from fasted morning training) triggers hormonal stress response even if dinner restores total daily balance
- **Recovery from chronic LEA**: menstrual cycle resumption can take 3-12+ months; not a quick fix
- **Diet duration limit** (Moore personal experience): no longer than ~8 weeks, max 6-8 lbs per cycle, then mandatory maintenance phase

### Platform Relevance

- **clinical**: RED FLAG -- detect declining performance + high training load + weight stability/gain pattern. Flag when performance regression coincides with maintained/increased training volume.
- **nutrition**: Track within-day energy distribution, not just daily totals. Flag fasted high-intensity training sessions.
- **training_load**: OTS vs RED-S differential -- if performance declines despite adequate rest periods, flag potential underfueling rather than overtraining.
- **blocks**: Enforce separation of weight-loss phases from high-intensity blocks. Never combine aggressive calorie restriction with VO2max or threshold blocks.

## 2. Perspectives #40 -- Energy Expenditure & Compensation (Eric Trexler)

**Guest**: Eric Trexler, postdoc at Herman Pontzer's lab (Duke), constrained energy expenditure model expert.

### Key Claims

| Claim | Evidence Level | Source |
|-------|---------------|--------|
| Constrained Energy Model: total daily energy expenditure does NOT scale linearly with activity level | **Strong** -- Pontzer lab, doubly-labeled water across populations | Pontzer 2016, DLW studies |
| Maximum sustainable metabolic scope is ~2.5x BMR long-term; can be higher short-term | **Strong** -- Thurber et al. 2019 (Race Across USA) | Thurber 2019 |
| Elite endurance athletes may push to ~3x BMR (biological variation) | **Moderate** -- observational | Moore's coaching model, Areta case study |
| Weight-stable athletes can still experience significant energy compensation (low T3, amenorrhea, reduced NEAT) | **Strong** -- bodybuilder + endurance literature | Trexler, Dulloo |
| Energy cost of tissue repair: rest-day expenditure was 3x BMR after ultra-running | **Moderate** -- small study, 2 participants | Recent ultra-running paper |
| Best predictor of weight regain = amount of fat-free mass lost | **Strong** | Dulloo et al. |
| Compensation: BMR reduction + NEAT reduction are primary culprits; exercise efficiency changes minor at higher intensities | **Strong** | Trexler, Pontzer systematic reviews |

### Actionable Guidelines

- **TDEE estimation**: track daily weight + consistent calorie intake for 2-4 weeks; calculate weight trend slope. Far more accurate than BMR calculator + bike kJ.
- **Compensation rule of thumb**: +100 kcal/day exercise may only yield +70 kcal TDEE (30% compensation at moderate levels); worse at extremes.
- **Wearable calorie estimates are unreliable** -- do not use as sole nutrition basis.
- **"Reverse dieting" signal**: if adding 200 kcal/day doesn't cause weight gain but restores hormonal markers, athlete was in compensated LEA.
- **Weight loss + resistance training**: always include strength work to minimize FFM loss (the #1 predictor of rebound).
- **Pro cycling "get in, get out"**: weight loss for competition should be brief and targeted; chronic low weight is uniquely destructive in cycling culture.

### Platform Relevance

- **nutrition**: Build TDEE estimation using energy-balance method (14-28 day rolling avg). Flag when estimated TDEE drops below predicted (metabolic adaptation signal).
- **clinical**: AMBER FLAG -- athlete reporting low energy, cold extremities, disturbed sleep, low libido while weight-stable = probable compensated LEA. Recommend calorie increase trial before assuming overtraining.
- **training_load**: Constrained model means training kJ is not a 1:1 energy expenditure proxy. Build compensation factor into nutrition recommendations.
- **blocks**: Separate weight loss blocks from high-intensity blocks.

## 3. TMT #55 -- When to Take a Rest Week / Trusting Subjective Metrics

**Hosts**: Kolie Moore + Coach Fabiano.

### Key Claims

| Claim | Evidence Level | Source |
|-------|---------------|--------|
| Planned rest weeks should vary by block intensity: 2-3 weeks for threshold+, 3-5 weeks for endurance/base | **Practitioner consensus** | EC coaching team |
| Sprint power and supra-threshold feel are the most sensitive freshness/fatigue indicators | **Moderate** -- physiological rationale (neural drive) | EC coaching |
| HRV trends useful during high-stress VO2max blocks but unreliable for low-intensity blocks | **Moderate** -- mixed literature | Fabiano's client data |
| Type-A athletes engage in self-deception about fatigue; subjective "I feel fine" cannot be trusted when performance data says otherwise | **Moderate** -- Trivers' self-deception research | Coaching experience |
| Frequent illness = strong retrospective indicator of insufficient rest weeks | **Strong** -- immunology of overreaching | Well-established |
| CTL does NOT equal fitness; peak performances often occur after CTL dips from peak | **Moderate** -- WKO observation | EC coaching |

### Actionable Guidelines

**Rest Week Decision Tree:**
1. **Threshold/VO2max blocks**: rest every 2-3 weeks
2. **Base/endurance blocks**: rest every 3-5 weeks
3. **Reactive triggers** (any one sufficient): performance regression, persistent low motivation, illness, failed mid-block workout + failed retest 2-3 days later
4. **If athlete asks for rest week**: ALWAYS grant it, 100% of the time

**Rest Week Structure:**
- Short, genuinely easy rides (IF < 0.50)
- Minimum 4-5 days; 7+ days after VO2max blocks
- End-of-rest test: sprint power + 5min at FTP; if both feel good, resume
- 10+ days fully off bike = measurable fitness loss

**Subjective Metrics to Track:** motivation to train (most reliable), mood/irritability ("ask your partner"), sleep quality, RPE drift at constant power, dissociated RPE (legs vs. lungs), brain fog

**When NOT to trust "I feel fine":** performance data shows regression, Type-A personality, high life stress + denial pattern

### Platform Relevance

- **training_load**: Rest-week recommendation engine -- intensity-weighted block duration triggers. After 2 weeks threshold+ or 4 weeks endurance, prompt rest assessment.
- **blocks**: Auto-adjust rest week by block type. VO2max blocks get 7+ day recovery.
- **clinical**: RED FLAG -- illness frequency > 1x per 6-8 weeks = chronic under-recovery. AMBER FLAG -- "radio silence" (no workout comments) = probable motivational collapse.
- **training_load**: "Performance vs. subjective" conflict detector -- if power trending down but athlete reports feeling good, flag for review. Data wins over feelings.

## 4. TMT #58 -- Why Rest Can Be So Scary

**Hosts**: Kolie Moore + Coaches Rory and Megan.

### Key Claims

| Claim | Evidence Level | Source |
|-------|---------------|--------|
| Athletes who don't rest settle into "middle intensity black hole" -- 80-90% capacity, never truly hard or easy | **Practitioner consensus** | EC coaching |
| End-of-rest sprint/1-min test often produces lifetime PRs | **Anecdotal but frequent** | EC coaching (Megan, Rory, Fabiano) |
| CTL drop during rest is cosmetic, not functional | **Moderate** -- WKO data | EC coaching |
| Stress dissipation after chronic overreaching takes days to weeks | **Moderate** | EC coaching |
| Rest week minimum dose should be >25% of normal volume | **Practitioner estimate** | EC coaching |
| Severely overtrained athletes may need 2-3 months off before productive training | **Practitioner consensus** | EC coaching |

### Actionable Guidelines

- **Fear of rest = signal rest is needed**: athletes most afraid to rest typically need it most
- **Openers at end of rest week**: 15min sweet spot or short efforts; "a privilege for the well-rested"
- **"I perform well fatigued" belief**: almost always false -- operating at 80-90% without realizing it
- **Rest week progression**: pure recovery 2-3 days, progress to endurance, test legs by day 5-7

### Platform Relevance

- **training_load**: Detect "intensity black hole" -- IF consistently 0.65-0.80, no true recovery (IF < 0.50), no true high-intensity. Flag as suboptimal distribution.
- **blocks**: Post-rest-week: insert opener rides before first hard workout. Auto-schedule sprint + FTP feel-check as gating workout.
- **clinical**: RED FLAG -- if rest period exceeds 2-3 weeks to restore baseline, flag as non-functional overreaching or RED-S.

## 5. TMT #48 -- Avoiding Over-Optimization

**Hosts**: Kolie Moore + full EC coaching team.

### Key Claims

| Claim | Evidence Level | Source |
|-------|---------------|--------|
| Consistency beats optimization: boom-bust cycle produces same annual training as moderate approach, with more misery | **Practitioner consensus** | EC coaching |
| Body has ONE pool for ALL stress (training + work + family + sleep debt) -- allostasis | **Strong** -- allostatic load theory | McEwen |
| Motivation is a physiological signal, not a character flaw | **Moderate** | EC coaching |
| "High Zone 2" generates significantly more fatigue than "low Zone 2," compromising interval quality | **Moderate** | EC coaching |
| Weight loss should NEVER coincide with high-intensity training blocks | **Moderate** | EC coaching + Trexler/Pontzer |
| Mid-season breaks (full week off May/June) are invaluable | **Practitioner consensus** | EC coaching |

### Actionable Guidelines

- **Minimum effective dose**: if life is chaotic, get on bike 4-5x/week for any duration; habit > plan perfection
- **Endurance pacing**: genuinely easy, conversational pace (low Zone 2), NOT top of endurance zone
- **Weight loss timing**: off-season or low-intensity phases ONLY
- **Cross-training**: strength (posterior chain, core), running, swimming for bone density + injury prevention

### Platform Relevance

- **training_load**: Allostatic load model -- integrate TSS with life-stress proxies (comment sentiment, frequency, missed sessions).
- **blocks**: Detect boom-bust CTL pattern (rapid ramp + crash, repeated annually). Flag and recommend moderate progression.
- **nutrition**: AMBER FLAG -- weight loss during high-intensity block.
- **training_load**: Zone distribution audit -- if >30% endurance time in upper Zone 2, flag as "endurance too hard."

## 6. TMT #51 -- RPE, Workout Feedback, Adaptation & Health

**Hosts**: Kolie Moore + Coach Fabiano.

### Key Claims

| Claim | Evidence Level | Source |
|-------|---------------|--------|
| Borg RPE (6-20) correlates linearly with HR; CR-10 follows exponential function aligned with lactate/pain | **Strong** | Borg 1964, 1982 |
| RPE calibration differs significantly between individuals (sprinters vs endurance) | **Moderate** | EC coaching |
| Best communicators = most successful coaching clients; workout metadata > power data alone | **Practitioner consensus** | EC coaching |
| VO2max improves similarly with endurance, HIIT, and sprint training across all ages, sexes, disease states | **Strong** -- systematic review | Cois et al. |
| HRV most useful during very high training loads (VO2max doubles); trends over 3-7 days, not single readings | **Moderate** | Fabiano's HRV data |
| Indoor training requires longer recovery than equivalent outdoor (thermoregulation strain) | **Moderate** | EC coaching |
| J-curve: optimal health at ~300 min/week moderate OR ~150 min/week vigorous + 2x/week strength | **Strong** | ACSM guidelines |

### Actionable Guidelines

**RPE System:** Use CR-10 (0-10). Calibrate against tests every 8-12 weeks. Dissociate leg RPE from cardio RPE. RPE drift at constant power = fatigue signal.

**Workout Feedback Essentials:** (1) Nutrition quality, (2) Sleep quality/duration, (3) Life stress 1-10, (4) Equipment changes, (5) Environment, (6) RPE for intervals + overall, (7) Anything unusual

**HRV:** Most useful during VO2max blocks with doubles. Look at 3-7 day trends. Indoor blocks show slower HRV recovery. Sustained depression >7 days = impending illness.

**Health:** VO2max 40+ is strong target; 50+ excellent. Most cyclists already exceed health dose.

### Platform Relevance

- **training_load**: Track RPE:power ratio. Rising RPE at constant power = fatigue accumulation.
- **clinical**: Structured feedback fields: sleep quality, nutrition quality, stress level, RPE (legs), RPE (overall).
- **blocks**: Prompt performance test every 8-12 weeks; flag when zones >90 days stale.
- **training_load**: Indoor recovery multiplier: 1.1-1.2x TSS from indoor sessions.

## Cross-Episode Synthesis: Platform Detection Rules

### RED FLAGS (Immediate Intervention)

| Signal | Detection Method |
|--------|-----------------|
| Performance declining + training load maintained + weight stable | Power trend + TSS trend + weight |
| Illness frequency > 1x per 6-8 weeks | Calendar annotations |
| Athlete requests rest week mid-block | Direct request |
| Recovery time progressively increasing | RPE recovery, HRV trends |
| Male: low libido/erections; Female: irregular/absent cycle | Self-report |

### AMBER FLAGS (Monitor Closely)

| Signal | Detection Method |
|--------|-----------------|
| Weight-stable + cold extremities, low off-bike energy, disturbed sleep, brain fog | Subjective feedback |
| "Intensity black hole": most rides IF 0.65-0.80, no true easy or hard | IF distribution |
| Weight loss during high-intensity block | Block type + deficit detection |
| Boom-bust CTL pattern | CTL time series |
| RPE at constant power trending up 2+ weeks | RPE:power tracking |
| HRV sustained depression >7 days | HRV trends |
| "Radio silence" -- no workout comments | Comment frequency |
| Endurance rides consistently IF > 0.70 | IF audit |

### Recovery Model Parameters

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| VO2max block max before mandatory rest | 2-3 weeks |
| Threshold block max before rest | 3 weeks |
| Base/endurance block max before rest | 3-5 weeks (6 for experienced) |
| Rest week minimum duration | 4-5 days (7+ after VO2max) |
| Rest week minimum activity dose | >25% normal volume, genuinely easy |
| Days off before measurable detraining | ~10 days |
| Indoor training recovery multiplier | 1.1-1.2x outdoor equivalent |
| Time to exit non-functional overreaching | 2 weeks to 3 months |
| Maximum sustainable metabolic scope (long-term) | 2.5x BMR (3x for elites) |
| Optimal EA for female athletes | >= 45 kcal/kg FFM/day |
| Critical EA threshold | < 30 kcal/kg FFM/day |
| Max safe diet duration per cycle | ~8 weeks |
| Performance test freshness interval | Every 8-12 weeks |
