# Q1: Stuck at 280W FTP for 6 Months Despite Consistent Threshold Work

## Short Answer

You have likely hit the FTP-from-threshold ceiling. More threshold work will only extend your TTE (time to exhaustion at 280W), not raise FTP itself. You need to raise your VO2max -- the aerobic ceiling that FTP grows into.

## Why This Is Happening

FTP training is **utilization training**: it maximizes how much of your existing aerobic capacity you can sustain. After 6 months of consistent threshold work, you have probably pushed your FTP close to its current VO2max-limited ceiling. "FTP training alone hits a ceiling -- VO2max work is needed to raise it further. VO2max raises the roof for FTP to grow into" (TMT-60 [E], wiki/ftp-threshold-testing). The specific trigger is: "FTP unchanged after threshold block + rest + retest" (TMT-60 [E], ec-master-reference).

Additionally, TTE stagnation at FTP specifically signals the need for VO2max work, not more threshold training (TMT-60 [E], wiki/vo2max-training).

## What to Change

**1. Introduce a VO2max block (3-6 weeks).** Start with classic long intervals and progress weekly:

| Week | Session | Details |
|------|---------|---------|
| 1 | 4 x 3 min @ 308-336W (110-120% FTP) | 3 min easy between |
| 2 | 5 x 3 min @ 308-336W | 3 min easy |
| 3 | 4 x 4 min @ 302-336W | 3 min easy |
| 4 | Rest week (IF < 0.50) | |
| 5 | 5 x 4 min @ 308-336W | 3 min easy |
| 6 | 4 x 5 min @ 302-336W | 4 min easy |

Target RPE: 9-9.5/10. High cadence (100-120 RPM) reduces muscular fatigue (WD-55 [R+E], wiki/interval-design). Limit to 1-2 VO2max sessions per week; more than 2 shows no added benefit and increases overtraining risk (Seiler 2010 [R], wiki/interval-design).

**2. Keep one threshold maintenance session every 1-2 weeks** at RPE 6-7/10 to preserve TTE (TMT-60 [E]).

**3. Protect your easy rides.** Target IF 0.50-0.65 on all other days. Riding endurance at IF 0.70-0.75 is the single most common amateur error and directly compromises interval quality (TMT-58 [E], TMT-69 [E], ec-master-reference).

**4. Rest week every 2-3 weeks** during the VO2max block, minimum 7 days easy (TMT-55 [E], wiki/vo2max-training).

**5. After the block: rest, then retest.** FTP often jumps without additional threshold work once the VO2max ceiling has been raised (TMT-60 [E], wiki/vo2max-training). A meaningful change is >6W / >2% (WD-62 [R]).

## What NOT to Do

- Do not increase threshold interval power above 280W to "force" a breakthrough. Threshold intervals progress in duration, not power (TMT-45 [E], wiki/interval-design).
- Do not panic-train by doubling VO2max sessions. The anti-panic protocol is clear: never double intensity to catch up (TMT-71 [E]).
- Do not judge progress by FTP alone. Race results can improve substantially with flat FTP through durability, repeatability, and specificity (TMT-70 [E], wiki/ftp-threshold-testing).
