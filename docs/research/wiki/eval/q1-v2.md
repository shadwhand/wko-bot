## Answer

Your plateau is textbook. Six months of threshold work with no FTP movement means you have maximized **utilization** of your current aerobic ceiling -- your VO2max is now the bottleneck, not your threshold. As Kolie Moore puts it, VO2max "raises the roof" so FTP has room to grow into. Continuing threshold work from here will only extend your Time to Exhaustion at 280W; it will not push 280W higher. [E] (TMT-60; wiki: FTP & Threshold Testing, VO2max Training)

**What to change -- three concrete steps:**

**1. Start a VO2max block (3-6 weeks).** Drop your second weekly threshold session and replace it with VO2max intervals. Begin with classic long intervals: 4x4 min at 115-120% FTP (~320-335W for you), 3 min easy recovery. Progress weekly by adding one rep or extending to 5 min. Aim for ~20 min total at intensity per session. RPE should be 9-9.5/10. [E] (WD-55, TMT-45; wiki: VO2max Training)

Take a rest week every 2-3 weeks during this block -- VO2max work has the highest recovery cost of any training. Rest-week rides should be IF < 0.50. [E] (TMT-55; wiki: Training Periodization)

**2. Audit your easy rides.** The most common amateur error is riding endurance days at IF 0.70-0.75 -- too hard for recovery, too easy for adaptation. This "intensity black hole" silently erodes your ability to absorb hard sessions. Target IF 0.50-0.65 on every easy day. If your endurance rides are averaging IF 0.68+, fixing this alone may unlock the plateau. [E] (TMT-58, TMT-69; wiki: FTP & Threshold Testing)

**3. Retest after the block.** After completing the VO2max block plus a full 7+ day rest week, retest FTP using a Kolie Moore protocol (ride to failure starting ~275W). FTP often jumps after a VO2max block without any additional threshold-specific work -- the higher ceiling gives your existing threshold fitness room to express itself. A meaningful change is >6W (>2% of 280W). [R] (WD-62, TMT-60, TMT-66; wiki: FTP & Threshold Testing)

**What NOT to do:** Do not add more threshold volume, do not switch to sweet spot hoping for a different result, and do not panic-train by doubling intensity across the board. The signal from your body is clear: the ceiling is the problem, not the floor. [E] (TMT-60, TMT-71; wiki: Training Periodization)
