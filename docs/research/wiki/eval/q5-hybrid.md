# Q5: What Is Durability, and How Is It Different From High FTP?

## Defining Durability

Durability is the ability to resist the decline in physiological function and power output over prolonged exercise [R]. A rider with high durability can sustain a larger fraction of their fresh-state power at hour 4, 5, or 6 than a rider with low durability -- even if both have the same FTP tested on fresh legs.

FTP represents maximum sustainable power for approximately one hour, tested from a rested state [R][E]. It is a snapshot of peak capacity. Durability describes how that capacity erodes under fatigue accumulation.

## Why the Distinction Matters

Consider two riders, both with 300W FTP:

- **Rider A (high durability):** After 4 hours of riding, can still sustain 270W (90% of fresh FTP) for a 20-min climb.
- **Rider B (low durability):** After 4 hours, can only sustain 240W (80% of fresh FTP) on the same climb.

On paper they are equal. In a 200km race, Rider A drops Rider B on every climb in the second half. Durability is the differentiator in long events -- gran fondos, ultra-races, multi-day stage racing [E].

## Physiological Underpinnings

Durability loss stems from several mechanisms [R][E]:

- **Glycogen depletion** -- as muscle glycogen falls, the body shifts toward fat oxidation, which produces fewer watts per liter of O2.
- **Neuromuscular fatigue** -- reduced motor unit recruitment and firing rate after hours of repetitive contraction.
- **Cardiovascular drift** -- cardiac output declines as core temperature rises and plasma volume drops, reducing O2 delivery.
- **Central fatigue** -- brain-mediated downregulation of effort as perceived exertion rises.

FTP testing captures none of these because it is performed fresh. A rider can have a high FTP and poor durability if their glycogen management, heat tolerance, or fatigue resistance is weak [E].

## How to Measure Durability

Practical markers include [E]:

- **Time to Exhaustion (TTE):** How long you can hold FTP before failing. Higher TTE = better durability.
- **Power-duration curve decay:** Compare your 20-min power fresh versus after 3-4 hours of riding. The delta reveals durability.
- **Decoupling:** Heart rate rising while power stays constant (aerobic decoupling > 5%) signals durability limits.

## How to Train Durability

Durability improves through different stimuli than FTP [E][R]:

1. **Long aerobic rides** (3-5+ hours at Z2) stress glycogen stores and train fat oxidation.
2. **Fueling practice** -- high carbohydrate intake (80-120g/hr) during long rides directly preserves glycogen and delays durability loss.
3. **Back-end intensity** -- threshold or sweet-spot efforts in the final 60-90 min of a long ride specifically train the fatigued-state performance.
4. **Heat acclimatization** -- reduces cardiovascular drift.

## Bottom Line

FTP is your ceiling on fresh legs. Durability is how much of that ceiling you retain over time. For any event longer than 2-3 hours, durability may matter more than raw FTP [E].
