# FTP Plateau at 280W: Breaking Through After 6 Months

A 6-month plateau at 280W FTP despite consistent threshold work is a classic case of monotonous stimulus. Your body has fully adapted to the training dose you are giving it. Here are concrete changes to make.

## 1. Reduce Threshold Volume, Add Polarized Intensity

Doing more threshold work when you are already adapted to threshold work produces diminishing returns. Research by Stoggl and Sperlich (2014) showed polarized training (80% easy / 20% hard) outperformed threshold-dominant models over 9 weeks in trained endurance athletes.

**Action:** Cut sweet-spot/threshold sessions from 3x/week to 1x. Replace the other two with one VO2max session and one endurance ride.

## 2. Add VO2max Intervals (106-120% FTP)

VO2max work drives central adaptations — stroke volume, cardiac output, oxygen delivery — that raise the ceiling above which FTP can climb. Billat et al. showed that time at VO2max is the key stimulus for improvement.

**Action:** 1x per week, perform 5x4min at 300-335W (107-120% of 280W) with 3-4min recovery at 140W. Progress to 6x4min over 4 weeks. Target 20-24 minutes of accumulated work above 106% FTP per session.

## 3. Incorporate Neuromuscular / Force Work

Short maximal efforts recruit high-threshold motor units that threshold riding largely ignores. Over time this improves peak power and fractional utilization.

**Action:** 1x per week include 8-10 x 30sec all-out efforts (targeting 450-550W+) with 4.5min full recovery. Alternatively, low-cadence force intervals: 6x3min at 280W but at 55-60rpm on a climb.

## 4. Increase Total Training Volume by 10-15%

If you are riding 8-10 hours/week, adding 1-1.5 hours of easy Z1/Z2 endurance riding (155-195W, under 70% FTP) provides aerobic base expansion without additional fatigue. Seiler's research consistently links higher training volume to higher FTP in trained athletes — provided the added volume is truly easy.

## 5. Periodize in 3-4 Week Blocks

Stop training the same way every week. Structure 3-week build blocks with a 4th recovery week at 60% of normal training stress. Each block should have a specific focus:

- **Block 1 (weeks 1-3):** VO2max emphasis — 2x VO2max sessions/week, 1x threshold
- **Block 2 (weeks 5-7):** Repeatability — over-unders at 260-300W (2min/2min), 4-5 sets
- **Block 3 (weeks 9-11):** Threshold extension — 2x20min at 275-285W progressing to 2x25min

## 6. Check Recovery Basics

Plateaus often have an off-the-bike component. Ensure 7-8 hours of sleep, adequate protein (1.6-2.0 g/kg/day), and sufficient daily calories. A chronic energy deficit of even 300-500 kcal/day suppresses adaptation.

## Expected Outcome

With these changes applied consistently for 8-12 weeks, a breakthrough of 8-15W (288-295W FTP) is a realistic target. The key insight: your body stopped adapting because you stopped changing the stimulus.
