# Q2 (Augmented): VO2max-FTP Relationship

**Question:** "What does the research say about the relationship between VO2max and FTP? Can I raise FTP without raising VO2max?"

---

## Answer

FTP and VO2max are linked but not locked together. FTP is expressed as a **percentage of VO2max** -- trained cyclists typically sustain 70-85% of VO2max at threshold [R] (vo2max-training, Section 2). This means FTP can improve through two independent mechanisms:

1. **Raise the ceiling (increase VO2max).** A higher VO2max creates room for FTP to grow into. This is the "raises the roof" principle from TMT-60 [E].
2. **Raise the floor-to-ceiling ratio (increase fractional utilization).** Threshold and sweet-spot training push the percentage of VO2max you can sustain closer to that 85% upper bound [R] (vo2max-training, Section 2).

**So yes, you can raise FTP without raising VO2max** -- but only up to a point. Threshold training extends TTE and improves lactate dynamics, both of which raise FTP at a fixed VO2max [E] (ftp-threshold-testing, "Threshold training is Utilization Training"). The Capacity vs. Utilization framework (Johnston, TP) makes this explicit: threshold work is utilization training that exploits existing aerobic capacity [R].

**The ceiling eventually matters.** When FTP has plateaued despite consistent threshold work + rest + retest, the signal is that utilization is maxed out and VO2max must increase [E] (TMT-60 decision tree; vo2max-training, Section 2). TTE stagnation at 60-75 minutes specifically indicates the need for VO2max work rather than more threshold volume [E] (TMT-60; ftp-threshold-testing, "When to Shift Away").

**VO2max is considerably less trainable than FTP.** Genetics and age account for at least 50% of VO2max, and improvements follow a logarithmic curve with roughly a 25% ceiling over untrained baseline [R] (WD-61). FTP work eventually stops raising VO2max entirely in mature athletes -- cross-stimulation disappears with training age [R] (WD-61). At that point, dedicated VO2max intervals (105-120% FTP, 4-5 min efforts) become the only lever for the ceiling [E] (WD-55).

**Important nuance:** even when VO2max does not change, performance can still improve through cycling economy, neuromuscular efficiency, and weight changes [R] (Baddick, TP). VO2max is only half the equation; efficiency is the other half [R]. Two riders with identical VO2max can perform very differently.

**Bottom line:** Early in your development, threshold work raises both FTP and VO2max simultaneously. As you mature, those pathways decouple. A stalled FTP despite solid threshold training is the clearest diagnostic signal to introduce VO2max blocks.
