# Durability in Cycling: Key Facts Summary

## Core Definition

**Durability** is the ability to maintain power output after accumulated work. It is *not* the same as FTP (Functional Threshold Power).

> "The time of onset and magnitude of deterioration in physiological characteristics over time during prolonged exercise." - Maunder et al. (2021) [R]

**Key distinction from FTP**: 
- **FTP** = sustainable power *at the start* of an effort (threshold capacity)
- **Durability** = ability to *retain* power *throughout* an effort as work accumulates

## Measurement Standard: kJ/kg

**Why kJ/kg, not raw kJ**: Normalizes for body weight (2000 kJ means vastly different things for 55kg vs 75kg athletes) [R] (van Erp 2021)

**Standard binning**: 10 kJ/kg intervals (10, 20, 30, 40, 50 kJ/kg) [R]

## Performance Benchmarks

| Category | Power Drop at 50 kJ/kg | Source |
|----------|----------------------|--------|
| **Elite pro** | < 2% | WD-60 [E] |
| **Strong amateur** | 2-10% | WD-60 [E] |
| **Good amateur** | 10-20% | WD-60 [E] |
| **Average amateur** | 20-40% | WD-60 [E] |

**Field test results** (1-min effort after 2-2.5hr ride):
- **< 1% drop** = Elite/pro
- **< 5% drop** = Very good
- **5-10% drop** = Good
- **10-15%+ drop** = Average [E] (Jakub Novak, ProCyclingCoaching)

**WKO5 case study** (Amber Neben, 50kg TT champion):
- Fresh 20-min power: 272W
- After 2000 kJ: 268W (-4W, ~1.5% drop)
- Compared to Cat 3 rider (65kg): 
  - Fresh: 274W
  - After 2000 kJ: 242W (-32W, ~11.7% drop) [E] (Tim Cusick)
