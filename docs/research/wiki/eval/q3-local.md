Based on the provided documentation, a 200km ride with significant climbing falls into the "Ultra Nutrition" category (specifically the 200km brevet transition zone). For a 78kg rider, here is the hour-by-hour fueling strategy:

### 1. Pre-Race Fueling
*   **Glycogen Loading (2-3 days prior):** Target 10-12 g/kg/day, which for a 78kg rider is **780-936 g of carbohydrates per day** [R] (nutrition-racing.md).
*   **Pre-Race Meal (3-4 hours before):** Consume **156-312 g of CHO** (2-4 g/kg body mass) [R] (nutrition-racing.md).
*   **Final 60 Minutes:** 30-60 g of CHO in the last 30 minutes is acceptable; avoid 75-150 g of simple sugars exactly 30-45 minutes before the start to prevent rebound hypoglycemia [R] (nutrition-racing.md).

### 2. During-Race Fueling Protocol
Since this is a long-duration event with high climbing, you should transition from engineered nutrition to real food to manage palatability and satiety.

| Phase | Duration | CHO Target | Primary Fuel Form | Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Phase 1** | 0-6 hours | 80-120 g/hr | Drink mix, gels, bars | Start fueling within the first 15-20 mins [R]. Use liquids/gels on the 3000m climbs (>85% FTP) [R]. |
| **Phase 2** | 6-12 hours | 60-90 g/hr | Mixed: Gels + Solid food | Transition to savory options (sandwiches, wraps, rice balls) as palatability fatigue sets in [E]. |
| **Phase 3** | 12+ hours | 40-70 g/hr | Real meals, savory foods | Focus on "real food" (pasta, soup, bread) to suppress ghrelin and maintain GI function [R][E]. |

### 3. Critical Execution Rules
*   **Frequency:** Feed every 15-20 minutes for solids/semi-solids and every 5-10 minutes for sips of drink mix [R][E] (nutrition-racing.md).
*   **Intensity Interaction:** On the steep climbs, favor liquids and gels. On the flats (60-75% FTP), utilize solid foods for satiety [R][E] (nutrition-racing.md).
*   **GI Protection:** Always consume gels with water to avoid hypertonic intake, which is the primary trigger for nausea [E] (nutrition-racing.md).
*   **Pacing for Nutrition:** If you are too fast to eat in the first hour, you are over-pacing [E] (pacing-strategy.md). If GI distress occurs, reduce intensity by 5-10% to improve splanchnic blood flow [R] (nutrition-ultra.md).

### 4. Post-Race Recovery
*   **Immediate (0-2 hours):** 1.0-1.2 g/kg/hr of CHO (**78-94 g/hr**) and 0.3-0.4 g/kg of protein (**23-31 g**) [R] (nutrition-racing.md).
*   **Daily Total:** If racing again the next day, target 8-12 g/kg/day of CHO [R] (nutrition-racing.md).
