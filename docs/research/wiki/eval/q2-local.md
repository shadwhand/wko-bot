**The Relationship Between VO2max and FTP**
*   **The "Ceiling" Principle:** VO2max sets the ceiling for FTP [E] (TMT-60). FTP is expressed as a percentage of VO2max, with typical trained cyclists operating at 70-85% of their VO2max at threshold [R].
*   **Mechanisms for Improvement:** To improve FTP, an athlete must either raise the VO2max (increasing the ceiling) or increase the fraction of VO2max sustainable at threshold (increasing utilization) [R].

**Raising FTP Without Raising VO2max**
Yes, it is possible to raise FTP without raising VO2max, but there are limits:
*   **Utilization Gains:** FTP can be improved by increasing the "utilization" of existing aerobic capacity [R].
*   **The FTP Ceiling:** FTP cannot rise indefinitely through threshold training alone [E] (TMT-60). Eventually, VO2max must increase to "make room" for further FTP growth [E] (TMT-60).
*   **Signals for VO2max Work:** If FTP plateaus despite consistent threshold training, or if TTE (Time to Exhaustion) stagnates at FTP, these are specific signals that VO2max work is required to raise the ceiling so FTP can grow further [E] (TMT-60).
