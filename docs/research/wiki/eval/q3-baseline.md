# Fueling Plan: 200km / 3000m Climbing

**Rider:** 78kg, FTP ~280W  
**Estimated duration:** 7-8 hours  
**Estimated avg power:** ~190-210W (IF ~0.70-0.75)  
**Estimated energy expenditure:** ~5,500-6,000 kJ (~1,300-1,400 kcal/hr equivalent)

---

## Pre-Ride (2-3 hours before)

- 150-200g carbohydrate-rich meal (rice, toast, banana, juice)
- 500ml water with electrolytes
- Avoid high fiber/fat to reduce GI risk

## Hour 1 (easy start, settle in)

- **Carbs:** 60g (1 gel + 1 bar or rice cake)
- **Fluid:** 500-750ml with sodium (~500mg Na)
- Start fueling at 20 minutes, not later. Glycogen sparing begins now.

## Hours 2-3 (building intensity, first climbs)

- **Carbs:** 80-90g/hr (push toward high end; mix glucose + fructose at 2:1 ratio)
- Per hour: 1 gel (25g) + 1 energy bar (40g) + 500ml carb drink (30g)
- **Fluid:** 500-750ml/hr depending on heat. Target 600ml in moderate conditions.
- **Sodium:** 500-700mg/hr (more if heavy sweater)

## Hours 4-5 (mid-ride, biggest climbs likely here)

- **Carbs:** 90-100g/hr. This is the critical window -- gut fatigue and glycogen depletion intersect. If you trained gut tolerance, push to 100g.
- Alternate textures: rice cakes, chews, gels. Palatability drops after hour 4.
- **Fluid:** maintain 500-750ml/hr. On long climbs, intake drops because bottles are harder to reach. Pre-drink at the base.
- Add caffeine: 100mg gel or cola at hour 4. Second dose at hour 6.

## Hours 6-7 (final third, fatigue management)

- **Carbs:** 80-90g/hr. Shift toward liquids and gels as chewing becomes harder.
- **Fluid:** 500ml/hr minimum. Dehydration accelerates perceived effort.
- **Caffeine:** second 100mg dose if not taken at hour 5.

## Final Hour (hour 7-8)

- **Carbs:** 60-80g. Gel + carb drink. Easy to consume, fast absorption.
- **Fluid:** 500ml. Finish strong; don't taper fluids.

---

## Totals

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Total carbs | 550-650g |
| Total fluid | 4-5.5L |
| Total sodium | 3,500-5,000mg |
| Caffeine | 200-300mg (hours 4-7) |

## Key Principles

1. **Train the gut beforehand.** 90-100g/hr requires practice. Build up over 4-6 weeks.
2. **Glucose:fructose ratio of 2:1** maximizes intestinal absorption beyond the ~60g/hr glucose-only ceiling.
3. **Front-load fueling.** You cannot catch up once you fall behind. The first 3 hours set the tone for hours 5-8.
4. **Sodium matters on climbs.** Low speed = less cooling = more sweat = more sodium loss. Supplement beyond what is in your drink mix.
5. **Caffeine timing:** delay until hour 4+ for maximum benefit in the final third when RPE is highest.
