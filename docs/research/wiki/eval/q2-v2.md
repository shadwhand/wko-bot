# Q2: What does the research say about the relationship between VO2max and FTP? Can I raise FTP without raising VO2max?

## The Relationship

FTP is expressed as a fraction of VO2max. Trained cyclists typically sustain threshold at 70-85% of their VO2max [R] (VO2max Training wiki, citing Santos/TP and EC podcast). This creates a hard constraint: if your VO2max ceiling is fixed, FTP can only rise by increasing the percentage of VO2max you can sustain -- and that fraction has a physiological upper bound.

This is the "raises the roof" principle (Empirical Cycling, TMT-60): VO2max sets the ceiling, and FTP lives inside it. Threshold training is "utilization training" -- it pushes FTP closer to the existing ceiling. VO2max training is "capacity training" -- it lifts the ceiling itself [E] (FTP & Threshold Testing wiki, citing TMT-60; training-for-the-uphill-athlete-capacity-vs-utilization-trai/TP).

## Can You Raise FTP Without Raising VO2max?

**Yes -- up to a point.** There are two distinct mechanisms for FTP improvement:

1. **Raise VO2max (higher ceiling).** More oxygen delivery and utilization capacity means a higher absolute power at threshold.
2. **Raise fractional utilization (same ceiling, higher percentage).** Improved lactate clearance, mitochondrial density, muscle fiber recruitment, and metabolic efficiency allow sustaining a greater share of VO2max.

Threshold-specific training (intervals at FTP with progressive duration: 4x10 -> 3x15 -> 2x20 -> 1x40+ min) extends TTE and pushes FTP upward without necessarily changing VO2max [E] (FTP wiki, TMT-45/TMT-60). Economy and neuromuscular efficiency improvements also raise FTP independently -- two athletes with identical VO2max can perform very differently based on efficiency (Baddick, TP) [R].

**However, this has a ceiling.** When FTP plateaus despite consistent threshold training and adequate rest, the diagnosis is clear: VO2max must increase for FTP to keep growing [E] (TMT-60). TTE stagnation at 60-75 minutes specifically signals this limit has been reached [E] (TMT-60). Continuing threshold work at this point only extends TTE further, which has diminishing returns beyond ~60-75 min.

## Evidence on Trainability

VO2max itself is considerably less trainable than FTP. Genetics account for at least 50% of VO2max; long-term improvements typically cap around 25% above baseline [R] (WD-61). Central factors (cardiac output, blood volume, hemoglobin mass) dominate VO2max -- one study showed that phlebotomy erased VO2peak gains back to baseline despite a 40% increase in mitochondrial density, confirming the supply side rules (WD-53) [R].

Early in training, cross-stimulation is high: sprint, threshold, and VO2max work all raise VO2max. This cross-stimulation disappears with training maturity (WD-61) [R]. Eventually, only targeted VO2max intervals (105-120% FTP, 4-5 min efforts, ~20 min total intensity per session) reliably lift the ceiling.

## Practical Takeaway

FTP can rise without VO2max gains through better utilization and efficiency -- this is how most athletes improve for years. But when FTP flatlines after a proper threshold block + rest + retest, the prescribed response is a VO2max block, not more threshold training. A higher ceiling creates room for FTP to grow into, and FTP often jumps after a VO2max block without any additional threshold-specific work [E] (TMT-60).
