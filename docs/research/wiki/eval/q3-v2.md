# 200 km / 3,000 m Fueling Plan (78 kg, FTP ~280 W)

## Estimated Ride Profile

At this distance and elevation, expect 7-8 hours of riding. Average power will likely sit around 180-210 W (0.64-0.75 IF), higher on climbs and lower on descents/flats. Total energy expenditure: roughly 5,000-6,000 kcal (Race-Day Nutrition; Ultra Nutrition, Sec 1).

## Pre-Ride (Morning of)

- **3-4 hours before start**: 2-3 g/kg CHO = 160-230 g carbs. Example: large bowl of rice with eggs, a banana, juice. Low fiber, low fat (Race-Day Nutrition, Sec 1 [R]).
- **15-20 min before start**: 1 gel (25-30 g CHO) + sips of drink mix.

## Hour-by-Hour On-Bike Plan

Target: **80-90 g/hr CHO** for the first 5-6 hours, tapering to **60-70 g/hr** in the final hours as GI fatigue sets in (Race-Day Nutrition, Sec 2; Ultra Nutrition, Sec 3 [R]).

| Hour | CHO (g) | Fluids (mL) | Sodium (mg) | What to Eat |
|------|---------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| 1 | 80-90 | 500-700 | 400-600 | Drink mix (40 g) + 1 gel + half a bar |
| 2 | 80-90 | 500-700 | 400-600 | Drink mix (40 g) + rice cake + 1 gel |
| 3 | 80-90 | 500-700 | 400-600 | Drink mix (40 g) + banana + 1 gel |
| 4 | 80-90 | 500-700 | 500-700 | Drink mix (40 g) + bar or sandwich half + 1 gel |
| 5 | 80-90 | 500-700 | 500-700 | Drink mix (40 g) + rice cake + 1 gel. Switch to savory if sweet is fading. |
| 6 | 70-80 | 500-700 | 500-700 | Drink mix (40 g) + wrap or onigiri. Solid food on flats, gels on climbs. |
| 7-8 | 60-70 | 500-700 | 500-700 | Drink mix + gels only if intensity is high on final climbs. Solid food if cruising. |

**Totals over ~7.5 hours**: ~550-650 g CHO (~2,200-2,600 kcal from carbs), 4-5 L fluid, 3,500-5,000 mg sodium.

## Key Tactical Rules

1. **Start eating at minute 15, not when hungry.** Gastric emptying is rate-limited; early intake ensures supply at hours 5+ (Race-Day Nutrition, Sec 2 [R]).
2. **Eat every 15-20 minutes** in small doses (~22 g per feed at 90 g/hr). Smaller, frequent doses absorb better and cause less GI distress than large boluses (Race-Day Nutrition, Sec 2 [R]).
3. **Solids on flats, liquids on climbs.** Above ~85% FTP (climbs), shift entirely to gels and drink mix -- splanchnic blood flow drops 60-80% at high intensity, impairing solid food digestion (Race-Day Nutrition, Sec 2 [R]).
4. **Always take gels with water** -- hypertonic intake without water is the number one nausea trigger (Race-Day Nutrition, Sec 6 [E]).
5. **Sodium matters at this duration.** At 500-700 mg/hr via electrolyte drink mix plus food, you will cover most losses for a 7-8 hour effort in temperate conditions (Hydration & Electrolytes, Sec 3 [R]).
6. **Caffeine**: 100-200 mg at hour 4-5 if needed for the final push (Ultra Nutrition, Sec 7 [R][E]).

## Post-Ride

Start recovery fueling within 30 minutes: 1.0-1.2 g/kg/hr CHO (78-94 g/hr) plus 20-30 g protein for the first 2 hours. Immediate post-ride fueling improves next-day performance even when total daily intake is matched (Race-Day Nutrition, Sec 4, WD-59 [R]).
