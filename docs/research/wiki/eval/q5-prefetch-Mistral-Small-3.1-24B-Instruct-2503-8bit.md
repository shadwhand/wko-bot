### Summary of Key Facts on Durability in Cycling and Its Difference from High FTP

#### Definition and Terminology

- **Durability** is the ability to maintain power output after accumulated work. It is not a new concept but has been measured since the advent of power meters in the late 1990s. The formal definition from **Maunder et al. (August 2021)** is "the time of onset and magnitude of deterioration in physiological characteristics over time during prolonged exercise." However, Kolie Moore argues that it is the deterioration of performance that reveals deterioration of measurements, not physiology deteriorating. [R] [E] (WD-60)

- **Four Overlapping Sub-Terms**:
  - **Durability**: Decline of CP, VO2max, etc., during steady power output.
  - **Fatigability**: Acute impairment of max power output post-kJ/kg.
  - **Repeatability**: Capacity to recover and reproduce high-intensity across bouts/stages/heats.
  - **Resilience**: Ability to resist fatigue and maintain performance; includes mental aspects.
  - Coaches generally treat these as a single degradation process and use kJ/kg as the unified catch-all metric. [E] (WD-60)

#### Controversy: Is Durability Overrated?

- **Evidence Base**: The evidence base for durability is surprisingly thin, and media interpretations are often stronger than the evidence suggests. The field is still in a definitional squabbling phase. [E] (WD-60)

- **Core Argument**: Over-indexing on durability without sufficient absolute power is a strategic error. You cannot "durability" your way to a result you lack the engine for. [E] (WD-60)

- **Goodhart's Law Warning**: Over-indexing on kJ/kg as a metric could lead to:
  1. Spending less energy in races to "protect" durability numbers.
  2. Only doing long easy rides (missing power development).
  3. Weight manipulation to improve kJ/kg ratios.
  4. Low-carb training for durability (explicitly dismissed). [E] (WD-60)

#### Measurement: kJ/kg as the Standard Unit

- **Why kJ/kg and Not Raw kJ**: Raw kJ is misleading across body weights. kJ/kg normalizes for body weight. Standard binning is 10 kJ/kg intervals (10, 20, 30, 40, 50 kJ/kg). [R] (van Erp 2021) [E] (WD-60)

- **Benchmarks**:
  - Elite pro: < 2% power drop at 50 kJ/kg.
  - Strong amateur: 2-10%.
  - Good amateur: 10-20%.
  - Average amateur: 20-40%. [E] (WD-60)

- **Practical Field Test**: Perform a max effort at a target duration after burning a set kJ amount. Compare to your fresh all-time best at that duration. [E] (TrainingPeaks / ProCyclingCoaching)

- **WKO5 Visualization**: WKO5 plots the Power Duration Curve after a specific energy expenditure. The standard view compares the fresh PD curve to the PD curve after X kJ of work. [E] (WD-60)

#### Measurement Caveats

- **Missing Fresh Baseline**: If your only max effort happened after 20 kJ/kg, that becomes your "0% loss" baseline, which is misleading. You need a genuine fresh effort within the recent past to anchor the comparison. [E] (WD-60)
