**From durability-fatigue.md:**

Definition:
- "The time of onset and magnitude of deterioration in physiological characteristics over time during prolonged exercise." [R] Maunder et al. (August 2021)
- Kolie Moore's critique: It's deterioration of *performance* that reveals deterioration, not physiology deteriorating. Your heart's capacity isn't impaired - the *ability to express performance* is lost. [E] (WD-60)
- Ability to maintain power output after accumulated work

Four sub-terms:
- Durability: Decline of CP, VO2max during steady power output
- Fatigability: Acute impairment of max power post-kJ/kg
- Repeatability: Capacity to recover and reproduce high-intensity
- Resilience: Ability to resist fatigue, includes mental aspects

**Key distinction from FTP:**
- FTP = what you can sustain fresh for ~60 minutes
- Durability = how much of your power you retain AFTER accumulated work
- "FTP stagnation does NOT mean fitness stagnation -- race results can improve substantially even with flat or declining FTP through durability, repeatability, race craft, and specificity improvements." [E] (TMT-70)


- TTE measures fresh threshold sustainability, while durability assesses power retention after work accumulation
- An athlete might maintain threshold for 55 minutes fresh but lose significant short-duration power after intense effort
