# Q3: Hour-by-Hour Fueling Plan for 200 km / 3,000 m

**Rider**: 78 kg, FTP ~280 W

## Estimating the Demand

At a sustainable 200 km pace with 3,000 m climbing, expect an average of ~190-210 W (roughly 0.68-0.75 IF). Using the energy expenditure formula `EE = Power x 0.8604 / GE` with GE of 23%, that yields approximately 710-790 kcal/hr (fueling-fundamentals, Sec 1). Total ride time: 7-8 hours. Total expenditure: ~5,500-6,000 kcal.

At 0.70 IF, the CHO fraction is roughly 0.65, meaning ~460-510 kcal/hr comes from carbohydrate -- about 115-128 g/hr of endogenous + exogenous CHO burn (fueling-fundamentals, Sec 2). You cannot replace all of that, but you can dramatically extend your glycogen runway.

## The Plan

**Pre-ride (3-4 hr before)**: 2-3 g/kg CHO = 160-230 g. Example: large bowl of rice + banana + sports drink. Familiar foods only (race-day-nutrition, Sec 1).

**Hour 0-1 -- Start immediately.** 80-90 g/hr. Begin within the first 15-20 minutes -- do not wait until hungry (race-day-nutrition, Sec 2). Use drink mix (40-50 g) + 1 gel (25 g) + 1 banana or bar (~25 g). Glucose:fructose ratio of 1:0.8 for maximal absorption (fueling-fundamentals, Sec 3). Fluids: 500-750 mL/hr. Sodium: 500-700 mg/hr.

**Hours 2-4 -- Steady state.** 80-90 g/hr. Continue the same cadence: ~22 g every 15 minutes. Mix forms -- drink mix as base layer, gels on climbs, bars/rice cakes on flats (race-day-nutrition, Sec 2). This is your highest-absorption window; GE begins declining ~0.5%/hr after hour 2, increasing metabolic cost (fueling-fundamentals, Sec 1).

**Hours 5-6 -- Gut fatigue onset.** Reduce to 70-80 g/hr if needed. Shift toward savory options if sweet fatigue appears (ultra-nutrition, Sec 3). Favor liquids and gels on climbs where intensity rises above 85% FTP, as splanchnic blood flow drops 60-80% at high intensity (fueling-fundamentals, Sec 3). Solid food on descents/flats.

**Hours 7-8 -- Final push.** 60-80 g/hr. Gels and drink mix only for the last hour if intensity rises for the finish. Caffeine: 100-200 mg gel or cola if needed (ultra-endurance, Sec 5).

|---|---|---|---|---|---|

**Total on-bike intake**: ~560-680 g CHO (~2,250-2,720 kcal). Combined with ~2,000-2,400 kcal starting glycogen (fueling-fundamentals, Sec 4) and substantial fat oxidation at this intensity, this plan avoids the bonk and preserves power for the final climbs.
