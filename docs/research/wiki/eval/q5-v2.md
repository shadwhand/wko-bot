# What Is Durability in Cycling, and How Is It Different from Just Having a High FTP?

## Definition

Durability is the ability to maintain power output after accumulated work. Formally defined by Maunder et al. (August 2021) as "the time of onset and magnitude of deterioration in physiological characteristics over time during prolonged exercise." Coaches have measured this concept since the late 1990s under names like "fatigue resistance" and "stamina" -- it predates the academic term by decades. [R] (Maunder 2021; [E] WD-60)

## How It Differs from FTP

FTP describes your maximum sustainable aerobic power in a fresh state. Durability describes how much of that power you retain after hours of riding. They are independent dimensions of fitness.

Tim Cusick's case study illustrates the distinction vividly. Amber Neben (world TT champion, 50 kg) and a Category 3 male rider (65 kg) had nearly identical fresh 20-minute power (~272W vs ~274W). After 2000 kJ of accumulated work, Neben lost only 4W (1.5% drop) while "Joe Rider" lost 32W (11.7% drop). Same FTP, radically different race outcomes at hour three. [E] (Cusick, TrainingPeaks)

The standard measurement unit is kJ/kg (van Erp et al., September 2021), binned at 10 kJ/kg intervals. Benchmarks from World Tour data and the Empirical Cycling podcast:

| Category | Power drop at 50 kJ/kg |
|----------|----------------------|
| Elite pro | < 2% |
| Strong amateur | 2-10% |
| Good amateur | 10-20% |
| Average amateur | 20-40% |

[E] (WD-60); [R] (van Erp 2021)

## The Critical Nuance: Absolute Power First

Van Erp's World Tour data shows successful climbers started at 6.28 W/kg (20 min) and dropped ~4% at 50 kJ/kg, while less successful climbers started at 5.99 W/kg and dropped ~9%. The better riders had both more power AND more durability. You cannot "durability" your way to a result you lack the engine for. [R] (van Erp 2021; [E] WD-60)

## Confounding Factors

Durability measurement is confounded by nutrition (under-fueling mimics poor durability), pre-effort intensity (2000 kJ of racing vs 2000 kJ of endurance riding produce different fatigue), and missing fresh baselines. The single most actionable lever for improving apparent durability is often fueling properly at 60-90 g/hr of carbohydrates. [E] (WD-60, TMT-73)

## Training Implications

Total time riding is the strongest predictor of durability -- not specific "durability workouts." The most effective specific methods are long rides (4-6+ hours monthly) and late-ride intervals (threshold/sweet-spot efforts after 2+ hours of endurance riding). [E] (WD-60, Cusick, Novak)
