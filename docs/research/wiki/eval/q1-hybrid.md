# Q1: Stuck at 280W FTP for 6 Months Despite Threshold Work

## The Problem: Threshold-Only Training Plateau

Six months of consistent threshold work without FTP movement is a classic signal that you are training the expression of fitness (FTP) rather than the underlying limiters that determine it. FTP is not a standalone physiological quality -- it sits downstream of VO2max, fractional utilization, and efficiency [R]. When all your training targets the threshold itself, you stop progressing because the ceiling above it (VO2max) and the floor beneath it (aerobic base) are no longer improving.

## Why This Happens

FTP is roughly 70-85% of VO2max for trained cyclists [R]. If your VO2max has plateaued, your FTP cannot rise further without either (a) raising VO2max or (b) improving the fraction of VO2max you can sustain. After months of sweet-spot and threshold-only work, both adaptations stall because you are not providing a sufficient stimulus at either end of the intensity spectrum.

The power-duration model makes this visible: if your curve has flattened in the 1-8 minute range while your 20-60 minute range stopped rising, the limiter is almost certainly aerobic ceiling, not threshold tolerance [E].

## What to Change

**1. Add VO2max intervals (2-3 weeks on, 1 week off).** Replace one threshold session per week with 4-6 x 4 min at 105-120% FTP, or 30/15 Billat-style intervals. These drive central adaptations (stroke volume, cardiac output) that raise the ceiling above FTP [R].

**2. Increase aerobic volume.** If your weekly hours allow it, add one longer Zone 2 ride. Polarized distribution (roughly 80% easy / 20% hard) consistently outperforms a threshold-heavy approach for breaking plateaus [R]. The extra volume improves mitochondrial density, capillarization, and fat oxidation -- all of which let you sustain a higher fraction of VO2max.

**3. Introduce variability.** Over-under intervals (alternating 10W above and below FTP in 2-min blocks) and race-simulation efforts create a novel stimulus that pure steady-state threshold work does not. Neuromuscular recruitment patterns change, and lactate clearance improves [E].

**4. Check recovery.** A 6-month plateau can also indicate accumulated fatigue or under-fueling. Ensure adequate sleep, caloric intake, and periodically de-load (every 3-4 weeks) [E].

## Summary

Stop hammering the threshold directly. Attack the limiter above (VO2max intervals) and below (aerobic volume), introduce session variability, and verify recovery is adequate. Most athletes who make this shift see FTP movement within 4-8 weeks.
