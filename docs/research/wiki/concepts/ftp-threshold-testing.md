# FTP & Threshold Testing

Compiled from EC podcast (53 episodes) and TrainingPeaks articles. Evidence-tagged.

Evidence levels: **[R]** = Research-backed, **[E]** = Experience-based, **[O]** = Opinion.

---

## Key Principles

- **FTP = MLSS (Maximal Lactate Steady State)** -- sustainable for 30-70 minutes depending on the athlete; it is NOT specifically "1-hour power." Individual TTE varies enormously. **[R]** (ec-master-reference / TMT-70, WD-62)
- **FTP stagnation does NOT mean fitness stagnation** -- race results can improve substantially even with flat or declining FTP through durability, repeatability, race craft, and specificity improvements. **[E]** (ec-master-reference / TMT-70)
- **Rolling PD curves from training data are more reliable than isolated test days** -- single tests carry ~2% power meter error, making 5W changes statistically meaningless. **[R]** (ec-master-reference / WD-62)
- **Ramp test improvements may reflect W' (anaerobic capacity), not VO2max** -- decompose PD curve changes into CP vs W' contributions to distinguish. **[R]** (ec-master-reference / WD-55)
- **FTP training alone hits a ceiling** -- VO2max work is needed to raise it further. VO2max "raises the roof" for FTP to grow into. **[E]** (ec-master-reference / TMT-60)
- **TTE stagnation at FTP signals need for VO2max work**, not more threshold training. **[E]** (ec-master-reference / TMT-60)
- **Maximum useful TTE is ~60-75 minutes** -- beyond this, the opportunity cost is too high; shift focus to raising FTP itself. **[E]** (ec-master-reference / TMT-60)
- **FTP determines all zone boundaries** -- an accurate FTP underpins TSS, IF, and the entire zone model. Stale FTP values corrupt the Performance Management Chart. **[E]** (using-threshold-improvement-notifications-in-the-e)
- **Threshold training is Utilization Training** -- it maximizes near-term performance by exploiting existing aerobic capacity. Capacity Training (volume, base) must precede it for long-term development. **[R]** (training-for-the-uphill-athlete-capacity-vs-utilization-trai)
- **A 20-minute all-out field test allows determination of FTP** -- representing the maximal watts sustainable for 60 minutes, when multiplied by 0.95. **[E]** (4-reasons-to-own-a-power-meter, two-winter-cycling-workouts-for-building-an-aerobic-base)
- **Indoor FTP differs from outdoor FTP** -- less muscle recruitment, fixed position, and thermal stress indoors typically produce lower numbers. Re-test after 4-6 weeks of indoor training to reset zones. **[E]** (indoor-cycling-physiological-benefits, gtn-presents-why-is-indoor-cycling-so-hard)
- **60 minutes of indoor riding approximates 90-100 minutes outdoors** due to continuous pedaling with no coasting. **[E]** (your-indoor-trainer-to-use-but-not-abuse)
- **Threshold power can be auto-detected** -- TrainingPeaks uses peak 60-min NP or 95% of peak 20-min average power (whichever is higher) to flag new thresholds from any ride. **[E]** (using-threshold-improvement-notifications-in-the-e)

## Testing Protocols

### 1. Standard 20-Minute Field Test (Coggan Protocol)

The most widely used FTP test. Simple but has known limitations.

| Step | Duration | Intensity | Notes |
|------|----------|-----------|-------|
| Warm-up | 15-20 min | Zone 1-2 | Progressive build |
| Blow-out | 5 min | Hard | Clear anaerobic contribution |
| Recovery | 5-10 min | Zone 1 | Full recovery |
| Test effort | 20 min | All-out, even pacing | Average power is the key metric |
| Cool-down | 10 min | Zone 1 | |

**FTP = 95% of 20-min average power.** The 5% discount accounts for anaerobic contribution in 20 minutes that cannot be sustained for 60 minutes. **[E]** (4-reasons-to-own-a-power-meter, two-winter-cycling-workouts-for-building-an-aerobic-base)

**Limitations:** The 0.95 multiplier is a population average. Individual FTP-to-20min ratios range from ~0.90 to ~0.98 depending on anaerobic capacity (W'). Athletes with large W' will over-estimate FTP with this protocol. **[R]** (ec-master-reference / WD-55)

### 2. Kolie Moore Protocol (EC Podcast Recommended)

A longer, sustained-effort test that directly measures FTP and TTE.

| Step | Duration | Intensity | Notes |
|------|----------|-----------|-------|
| Warm-up | 20-30 min | Progressive | Include 1-2 sub-threshold efforts |
| Test effort | Ride to failure | Start conservative at estimated FTP | Hold the highest sustainable power |
| Cool-down | 10-15 min | Zone 1 | |

**FTP = Average power of the sustained effort.** TTE = duration of that effort (typically 30-70 min). No correction factor needed because the test directly measures the longest sustainable power. **[E]** (ec-master-reference / TMT-60, TMT-66)

**Advantages over 20-min test:** Directly measures both FTP and TTE. No population-average correction factor. Reveals individual FTP/TTE relationship. **[E]**

**Key instructions:** Start 5-10W below estimated FTP and hold as long as possible. Failure should feel like a gradual inability to sustain, not an explosion. Test is invalid if effort collapses abruptly (indicates starting power was too high). **[E]** (TMT-66)

### 3. Ramp Test

**Not recommended for FTP estimation.** Improvements in ramp test power may reflect increased W' (anaerobic capacity) rather than true threshold gains. Ramp tests are useful for estimating VO2max but poor for FTP. **[R]** (ec-master-reference / WD-55)

### 4. Rolling PD Curve Estimation (Platform Method)

Uses training and racing data continuously rather than isolated tests. The platform `rolling_ftp()` function computes a 90-day rolling window, fitting the full PD model to extract mFTP.

**Advantages:** No dedicated test day needed. Captures true race-context performance. Avoids ~2% single-test error by aggregating many data points. **[R]** (ec-master-reference / WD-62)

**Limitations:** Requires sufficient near-threshold efforts in the window. Under-estimates if training has been entirely sub-threshold. **[E]**

### 5. TrainingPeaks Auto-Detection

Uses: `max(NP 60min, 0.95 * avg 20min peak power)` to auto-detect threshold improvements from any workout or race. **[E]** (using-threshold-improvement-notifications-in-the-e)

### Pre-Test Rest Requirements

- **3-5 days easy before a formal test** -- the most common testing error is insufficient pre-test rest. **[E]** (ec-master-reference / TMT-66)
- After a rest week, formal tests yield the most reliable numbers.
- Avoid testing after overreaching blocks -- perceived FTP decline may be fatigue, not fitness loss.

## Training at Threshold

### Interval Progression (EC Podcast Recommended)

Threshold intervals should progress in **duration**, not power. Power stays at FTP; the adaptation is extending TTE.

| Phase | Format | Total Time at FTP | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------------------|-------|
| 1 | 4 x 10 min | 40 min | 5 min recovery between |
| 2 | 3 x 15 min | 45 min | 5-8 min recovery |
| 3 | 2 x 20 min | 40 min | 8-10 min recovery |
| 4 | 2 x 25 min | 50 min | 10 min recovery |
| 5 | 1 x 40+ min | 40+ min | Continuous effort |

**Source:** TMT-45, TMT-60 **[E]**

**Optimal threshold interval format:** 3 x 20 or 2 x 30 min at FTP. **[E]** (ec-master-reference / TMT-60)

### Sub-Threshold / Sweet Spot Work

| Format | Intensity | Use Case | Source |
|--------|-----------|----------|--------|
| 2 x 20 at 92-95% FTP | Sweet spot | LT building, base phase | prepare-century-ride, your-indoor-trainer-to-use-but-not-abuse **[E]** |
| 3 x 10 at 100-107% FTP | At/above FTP | Mid-intensity phase | your-indoor-trainer-to-use-but-not-abuse **[E]** |
| 4-5 x 5 at 110-118% FTP | Supra-threshold | High-intensity phase | your-indoor-trainer-to-use-but-not-abuse **[E]** |
| 15 min at 89-94% FTP | Sweet spot | Aerobic base with structure | two-winter-cycling-workouts-for-building-an-aerobic-base **[E]** |

### Over/Under Intervals

Riding below and above threshold to teach lactate clearance -- mimics surges in road racing. Typically 1-2 min under (90-95% FTP) alternating with 1-2 min over (105-110% FTP) for 10-20 min blocks. **[E]** (4-reasons-to-own-a-power-meter)

### Bridging-the-Gap Workout (Race Specificity)

From how-to-move-up-a-category-in-road-cycling **[E]**:

| Segment | Duration | Intensity |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| Block | 5 min total | Repeats |
| Part A | 3 min | 105% FTP |
| Part B | 1 min | 110% FTP |
| Part C | 1 min | 125% FTP |
| Recovery | 5 min | Zone 1 |
| Reps | 6-8 x | |

### Stacked Threshold Workouts (Time-Crunched)

For athletes with limited training time. From maximize-each-hour-of-training-with-these-7-tips **[E]**:

- **45 min bike + 15 min run:** 5 x 5 min at FTP (1 min recovery) on bike, progressive run
- **60 min bike + 15 min run:** 3 x 10 min at FTP (3 min recovery), progressive run off bike

### Phased Indoor Threshold Program

From your-indoor-trainer-to-use-but-not-abuse **[E]**:

| Phase | Duration | Focus | Key Session |
|-------|----------|-------|-------------|
| 1 (LT Building) | 4-6 weeks | Sub-threshold | 2 x 20 at 92-95% FTP |
| 2A (Mid-Intensity) | 4 weeks | At threshold | 3-4 x 10 at 100-107% FTP |
| 2B (Mid-Intensity) | 2 weeks | Supra-threshold | 4-5 x 5 at 110-118% FTP |
| 3 (Peak) | 3-4 weeks | Mixed | Ladder: 5min + 2min + 1min efforts |

### When to Shift Away from Threshold Work

| Signal | Action | Source |
|--------|--------|--------|
| FTP unchanged after threshold block + rest + retest | Begin VO2max block | TMT-60 **[E]** |
| TTE stagnating at 60-75 min | Shift to raising FTP via VO2max | TMT-60 **[E]** |
| Race results improving despite flat FTP | Continue current approach | TMT-70 **[E]** |
| CTL rising but performance declining | Rest, not more training | TMT-72 **[E]** |

## Aerobic Threshold (AeT) and Its Relationship to FTP

The aerobic threshold (AeT, or LT1) is the first metabolic threshold -- the highest intensity at which fat and carbohydrate are burned in roughly equal parts. It sets the floor for sustainable endurance pacing. **[R]** (why-you-should-target-your-aerobic-threshold, zone-2-training-fat-burning)

| Parameter | Value | Source |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| AeT as % of max HR | 75-80% | why-you-should-target-your-aerobic-threshold **[E]** |
| AeT as % of FTP | 80-90% (varies with aerobic fitness) | why-you-should-target-your-aerobic-threshold **[E]** |
| LT1 true range | 45-85% FTP (far wider than most zone models assume) | TMT-69 **[E]** |
| Training to improve AeT | Long rides well under AeT | TMT-73 **[E]** |

**AeT interval progression** from why-you-should-target-your-aerobic-threshold **[E]**:
1. 6-8 x 5 min (1 min recovery)
2. 3-6 x 10 min (2 min recovery)
3. 3-5 x 15 min (3 min recovery)
4. 3-4 x 20 min (5 min recovery)
5. 2-3 x 30 min (recovery as needed)

**Key insight from EC:** "Zone 2 has nothing special about it" -- total volume matters more than sitting in a specific zone. LT1 rises best from volume well under it, not from training at LT1 specifically. **[E]** (ec-master-reference / TMT-69, TMT-73)

**Key insight from TP:** Efficient fat burning at AeT is what separates Grand Tour contenders from domestiques. Riders with identical FTP and VO2max differ in glycogen preservation ability. **[E]** (zone-2-training-fat-burning)

## Common Mistakes

1. **Over-testing** -- using formal tests instead of progressive workout performance as the fitness indicator. Test every 3-4 months, not every few weeks. **[E]** (ec-master-reference / TMT-66)

2. **Insufficient rest before testing** -- need 3-5 days easy. The single most common testing error. **[E]** (ec-master-reference / TMT-66)

3. **Erg mode hiding true readiness** -- free-ride mode reveals daily capacity. Wean off erg for key sessions. **[E]** (ec-master-reference / TMT-73)

4. **Mono-metrically focusing on FTP** -- race results, not FTP, are the ultimate metric. Athletes can improve substantially with flat FTP through durability and repeatability. **[E]** (ec-master-reference / TMT-70)

5. **Forcing power up instead of extending duration** -- threshold intervals should progress in duration at FTP, not by raising the target wattage. **[E]** (ec-master-reference / TMT-45)

6. **Switching power meters and expecting continuity** -- single to dual-sided can show zero FTP gain even with massive improvement. **[E]** (ec-master-reference / TMT-70)

7. **Using ramp test results to set FTP** -- ramp improvements may reflect anaerobic capacity, not threshold. **[R]** (ec-master-reference / WD-55)

8. **Applying the 0.95 correction factor universally** -- individuals with large W' will over-estimate FTP from a 20-min test. Those with small W' may under-estimate. **[R]** (ec-master-reference / WD-55, WD-62)

9. **Training in the intensity "black hole"** -- riding most sessions at IF 0.65-0.80, too hard for recovery and too easy for adaptation. The most common amateur error. **[E]** (ec-master-reference / TMT-58, zone-2-training-fat-burning)

10. **Not re-testing after moving indoors** -- indoor FTP is typically lower due to fixed position and less muscle recruitment. Using outdoor FTP for indoor zones leads to under-recovery. **[E]** (indoor-cycling-physiological-benefits)

11. **Rapidly increasing training volume** -- no more than 10% volume increase per week. A 75% overnight increase (common when schedule opens up) carries high injury risk. **[E]** (why-you-should-think-twice-before-increasing-training-volume)

12. **Riding endurance too hard** -- the #1 most common amateur error across every coaching episode. Target IF 0.50-0.65 for endurance rides, not 0.70-0.75. **[E]** (ec-master-reference / TMT-69, zone-2-training-fat-burning)

13. **Ignoring FTP staleness in PMC analysis** -- TSS and IF depend on an accurate FTP. An out-of-date FTP corrupts CTL, ATL, and TSB, making the entire Performance Management Chart unreliable. **[E]** (using-threshold-improvement-notifications-in-the-e)

### How the Platform Should Apply These Findings

1. **Default to `rolling_ftp()` for continuous monitoring** -- avoid reliance on isolated test days. Surface the rolling FTP trend on the dashboard.
2. **Decompose PD changes into CP vs W' contributions** -- when FTP changes, show whether the shift came from threshold or anaerobic capacity.
3. **Flag stale FTP** -- if last FTP test or significant near-threshold effort is >90 days old, warn that zone-derived metrics (TSS, IF, CTL) may be unreliable.
4. **Indoor/outdoor FTP split** -- maintain separate FTP values for indoor and outdoor training if the athlete regularly trains in both contexts.
5. **Surface TTE alongside FTP** -- TTE is the missing dimension. Two athletes at 300W FTP with TTE of 35 min vs 65 min have fundamentally different capabilities.
6. **VO2max block recommendation** -- when `rolling_ftp()` flatlines for >8 weeks despite consistent threshold training, suggest a VO2max block.
7. **Test protocol recommendation** -- suggest Kolie Moore protocol over 20-min test. Use `extract_ftp_test()` to auto-detect test rides.
8. **Endurance ride validation** -- `validate_endurance_rides()` should flag rides above IF 0.65 as potential intensity black-hole sessions.

## Conflicts & Caveats

### Where Sources Disagree

1. **0.95 multiplier universality** -- TrainingPeaks articles and Coggan's original work present 95% of 20-min power as standard FTP estimation. EC podcast (WD-55, WD-62) explicitly warns this is a population average and individual ratios range from ~0.90 to ~0.98. The platform should use the Kolie Moore protocol or rolling PD curve as primary, with 20-min test as fallback only.

2. **Indoor vs outdoor FTP magnitude** -- TP articles suggest indoor FTP is lower (indoor-cycling-physiological-benefits), but the magnitude varies widely. Some athletes show no difference on smart trainers; others show 5-15% gaps. The platform should support separate indoor/outdoor FTP values rather than applying a fixed correction.

3. **Sweet spot vs threshold priority** -- TP articles frequently recommend sweet spot (88-93% FTP) as the primary builder (prepare-century-ride, two-winter-cycling-workouts). EC podcast uses sweet spot as a stepping stone but emphasizes that true threshold work (100% FTP) with duration extension is the primary adaption driver (TMT-60). These are not contradictory -- sweet spot is appropriate early in a block, transitioning to full threshold as fitness permits.

4. **High-intensity as FTP builder** -- The "Miracle Intervals" article (all-out-miracle-intervals) showed 8% 60-min power improvement from short sprints alone. This challenges the assumption that only threshold-specific work raises FTP. EC podcast acknowledges VO2max raises the FTP ceiling (TMT-60), and short intervals likely improved VO2max/neuromuscular power. Not a direct conflict, but practitioners should note that FTP can improve via indirect pathways.

5. **Volume vs intensity for time-crunched athletes** -- 4-keys-to-success-for-time-crunched-athletes recommends heavily intensity-weighted programs (4 days/week, mostly VO2max and threshold). EC podcast (TMT-69, TMT-73) emphasizes that LT1/durability gains require volume. For ultra-endurance cyclists, the EC approach takes priority; for criterium racers, the time-crunched model is defensible.

### Unresolved Questions

- **Optimal FTP test protocol for ultra-endurance athletes** -- should TTE targets be >60 min, requiring modified test protocols?
- **FTP decay rate during detraining** -- limited quantitative data on how quickly FTP drops during time off (EC notes 10+ days for measurable detraining, TMT-55).
- **Power meter brand/type effect on FTP** -- switching from single-sided to dual-sided, crank to pedal, etc. can shift reported FTP by 3-8%. No standardized correction factors exist.
- **Altitude correction for FTP** -- FTP declines with altitude but individual responses vary. The platform does not yet model altitude-adjusted FTP.
- **Age-related FTP trajectories** -- how FTP ceiling and TTE capacity change across decades of training. Limited longitudinal data.
