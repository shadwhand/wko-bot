### Pinnacles 600k -- 608 km

- **Date:** 2023-04-22
- **Distance:** 608.0 km | **Elapsed:** 32.3 hr
- **Power:** IF 0.61, RP 169 | **Calories:** 11,638 kcal
- **Peak powers:** 1hr 184W, 2hr 179W, 4hr 166W
- **Zone distribution:** 48% Z1, 30% Z2
- **Route:** San Jose -> Hollister -> Paso Robles -> Santa Maria -> Santa Barbara -> return

**Ride report (athlete's notes):**

This was the first 400km+ ride in 4 years without any nausea -- a breakthrough. The athlete attributes this to improved nutrition protocol.

**Route highlights:**
- Hollister to Airline Highway: nice roads, respectful drivers, group gelling well
- Peach Tree to Paso Robles: time-trialed this section; cobbles near Paso caused comfort issues with hands (potential blisters) and chamois (too thin on Maloja bibs)
- Ate a Chipotle rice bowl (50/50 brown/white rice, corn salsa, salsa fresca, chicken) -- estimated 50g CHO from that stop alone
- Paso Robles to Santa Maria: minimal stops, flat from Cambria on. Group member got a nail flat 5 miles from Santa Maria. Hit Santa Maria at 16 hours.
- Recovery: chocolate milk + rice bowl, slept, planned 2am departure but delayed to 3:15am due to group member's morning nausea
- Day 2: Foxen Canyon Road (where Tadej Pogacar is KOM), Starbucks breakfast burrito in Buellton, In-N-Out burger near Ventura for final fuel

**Detailed nutrition log:** [E]

Target: 75-80g CHO/hour. Trained gut recently and wanted to push intake. Worked very well.

| Category | Items |
|----------|-------|
| **Real food** | Chipotle rice bowl, In-N-Out burger + fries + Coke, Starbucks breakfast burrito, chocolate croissant |
| **Drink mix** | 3x Skratch Superfuel (105g CHO), 4x half-mix Superfuel (53g), 3x Geluminati (62g) |
| **Gels** | 2x Maurten Gel 100 Caffeine (25g), 5x Maurten Gel 100 (25g), 2x SIS Beta Fuel (40g), 1x SIS Isotonic Caffeine (22g) |
| **Bars/snacks** | 8x Maurten Solid 225 (34g each), 4x Crispy Rice Cakes (35g each) |
| **Water** | 7,000 mL total |

Key nutrition strategies:
1. Supplement salt and eat at the first sign of gut discomfort -- took salt tablet + gel when temp hit 95F+ past Paso Robles, prevented stomach issues [E]
2. Never stop eating before a control. Always be eating. [E]
3. Rice is the perfect mid-ride meal. [E]
4. Pre-ride: 150-180g of breakfast + full bottle of Superfuel [E]

**Equipment notes:**
- MAAP Evade Pro LS Jersey: perfect for hot weather, no heat issues in Paso Robles. Will buy more colors. [E]
- Maloja Bibs: good for training, bad for 600k. Interface with Mirror Saddle was poor. Decision: Rapha Pro bibs for PBP. [E]
- Ornot Trail Magic Polartec Alpha insulating layer: excellent warmth and packability [E]
- USWE hydration pack: no carrying capacity beyond water, but adequate. Used bottles for drink mix, pack for plain water/electrolyte. [E]

> "What's funny about riding a route 4 years later is your memories of the route are definitely colored by nostalgia goggles. I distinctly remembered 101 being a little shorter from Gaviota into Santa Barbara, I thought the cobbles near Paso Robles were also shorter, and I don't remember the slog through Oxnard being as annoying."

### Surf City 600k DNF -- 499 km (of 600)

- **Date:** 2024-06-22
- **Distance:** 499.1 km | **Elapsed:** 21.6 hr (DNF at 83%)
- **Power:** 144W avg, 169W NP | **HR decoupling:** 28.6%
- **Route:** Santa Cruz -> Half Moon Bay -> La Honda -> Salinas Valley -> King City -> San Ardo -> return

**Root cause: hypothermia from insufficient clothing -> cascade failure.** [E]

The fitness and fueling were excellent. This was purely an equipment/thermoregulation failure. The athlete was on course for sub-24 hours (estimated 23:30 finish).

**Chain of events (athlete's Strava notes):**

> "Physically, I had the sub-24 hour in the bag."

> "I was well on course to hit 23:30 yesterday. Nutrition was going well -- was easily hitting 70-80g of carbs with no discomfort. Lunch and dinner were eaten and digested exactly as planned, and everything felt great at the turnaround point (200k to go)."

> "The first indication that something was happening was after taking a short refill at around 250 miles. As soon as I stepped out of the gas station, it was chilly and so I threw on all the clothes I had with me (long sleeve summer weight jersey, wind blocking jersey). Unfortunately I had chosen not to bring my down jacket, and I had forgotten my LS gloves at home. I also did not bring tights, which I had considered doing for a few days but decided against due to weight concerns."

> "With about 150 km to go is when I started facing severe issues. Due to an ability to maintain my body temp, particularly with a small cold headwind coming in front the Monterey Bay, I started shivering pretty severely any time I basically stopped pedaling. I also started dealing with some nausea, which got progressively worse as I became colder."

> "I was also falling asleep as I got colder and finally succumbed to lying down for a 15 min ditch nap. That turned into a 30 min ditch nap. Got up, tried to get warm by wrapping my short sleeve jersey around my hands and proceeded along River Road (final big stretch before making my way up the peninsula). I still couldn't keep warm, I couldn't keep my nutrition up, couldn't keep my power up."

**Power collapse data (from DNF forensics):**

| Phase | km | Power | HR | Temp | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Night start | 0-50 | 172W | 137 | 10C | Too fast (IF ~0.70) |
| Coast climbs | 50-150 | 150W | 136 | 11->37C | Peak heat at km 146 |
| Afternoon | 200-350 | 135W | 129 | 20C | Power fading |
| Cracking | 400-450 | 118W | 118 | 16C | Cold, sleepy, nauseous |
| Collapse | 450-500 | 48W | 96 | 12C | DNF |

**Lessons (athlete's own):** [E]
1. Bring warm clothes for the night: thermal bibs/tights, insulation vest (Ornot Alpha), warm gloves, beanie/neck gaiter
2. Completely dry off and get warm before starting again -- even if it means sacrificing 30-40 min
3. Don't chase the clock at the expense of thermoregulation
4. 80-100g CHO/hour is the right target -- nutrition was not the issue
5. Hypothermia causes nausea (not glycogen depletion in this case) -- warm first, eat second
6. The difference between DNF and finish is 500g of warm clothing

**Food plan that worked before the DNF:**
- Poke bowl in Marina (edamame rice bowl)
- Breakfast burrito in Pescadero
- Night meals: coffee + remaining food from bag

> "I think if I can keep my calories up (because I noticeably bonked slightly past Santa Rosa), I might be able to get handle on this. The Surf City 600k later confirmed that cold-induced nausea is a separate mechanism from fueling-related nausea.

## Recurring Patterns & Lessons

### Nutrition [E]

**What works:**
- 75-80g CHO/hour for rides under 24hr; 60-70g/hr acceptable for multi-day when GI stress accumulates
- Rice-based meals as primary real food (Chipotle rice bowls, onigiri, poke bowls)
- Breakfast burritos at dawn controls
- Skratch Superfuel as primary drink mix with plain water/electrolyte in hydration pack
- Proactive salt supplementation in heat (salt tablet + gel at first sign of gut discomfort)
- "Always be eating" -- never stop intake before a control
- Pre-ride: 150-180g CHO breakfast + full bottle of drink mix

**What failed:**
- Under-fueling -> bonk -> nausea spiral (Hopland 400k 2023)
- In heat (>32C), solid food absorption drops. Switch to liquids only.

**Nausea history:** Previously a recurring issue on 400km+ rides. Two distinct causes identified:
1. **Fueling-related:** Under-eating leads to low blood sugar -> nausea. Fix: maintain CHO intake proactively (validated at Pinnacles 600k). [E]
2. **Cold-induced:** Hypothermia -> nausea independent of fueling status. Fix: warm first, eat second. Do not try to ride through it. (validated at Surf City 600k DNF). [E]

### Temperature & Clothing [E]

This is the single biggest recurring failure mode. Three separate incidents:

1. **2023-07-22:** "Too hot. Had to cut ride short" -- heat forced route cut
2. **2024-06-22:** Surf City 600k DNF -- hypothermia from insufficient night clothing, cascading into nausea -> inability to eat -> power collapse -> DNF at 499km
3. **2023-03-25:** Hopland 400k -- coldest edition, underdressed in Petaluma

**Current night kit standard (post-DNF):** Ornot Polartec Alpha vest, thermal bibs/tights, warm long-sleeve gloves, beanie/neck gaiter. Total weight ~500g. Non-negotiable on any ride that includes night hours.

**Rule:** Layer up BEFORE you feel cold. At the last control before nightfall, full night kit goes on. The 500g of clothing is cheaper than a DNF.

### Pacing [E]

**Year-over-year progression is clear:**
- Healdsburg 300k: 4hr power 167W (2023) -> 171W (2024) -> 198W (2025)
- Del Puerto 200k: 4hr power 180W (2023) -> 183W (2024) -> 203W (2025)
- 1200km events: 4hr power 122W at PBP (2023) -> 154W at Hokkaido (2025)

**Pacing evolution:**
- Early rides (2023): higher Z1 percentages, more conservative but less efficient
- Mid rides (2024): shift toward Z2 dominance (50% Z2 on Healdsburg 2024)
- Recent rides (2025-26): higher sustained power with better zone discipline

**IF targets by distance:**
- 200k: 0.72-0.76 (tempo territory, aggressive but sustainable)
- 300k: 0.64-0.72 (moderate, Z2-heavy)
- 600k: 0.61 (Pinnacles) -- Z1/Z2 dominant
- 1200k: 0.49-0.52 -- survival pacing, Z1 dominant

**Starting pace rule:** First 50-100km should feel easy. The Surf City 600k DNF started at IF 0.70 for the first 50km -- too hot. Cap at 0.60 for 600k+.

### Equipment [E]

**Settled kit:**
- Rapha Pro bibs for events (chamois comfort on 600k+; Maloja rejected after Pinnacles)
- MAAP Evade Pro LS jersey for warm weather
- Ornot Polartec Alpha insulating layer (packable, effective)
- Mirror saddle (works with right bibs)
- Power meter (data-driven pacing)

**Ongoing lesson:** Don't over-pack on 1000k+ (SBS lesson), but DO bring the night kit on any ride with overnight hours (Surf City lesson). The balance is: minimal but complete.

### Sleep & Fatigue [E]

- Ditch naps become counter-productive when cold -- you get colder and can't rewarm (Surf City DNF)
- Back-to-back training weekends (300k + 200k, June 2023) are effective PBP prep
- On 1200k events, expect 60-67hr elapsed with sleep stops built in
- Energy dip at 2-4 AM is normal -- ride through it with caffeine, don't stop unless warming is needed
