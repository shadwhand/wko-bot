### U23-to-Pro Transition Benchmarks

From Nate Wilson's analysis coaching Will Barta to a World Tour contract:

- **"Tired 20" minimum**: 5.5-5.8 W/kg for 20 min after 2500 kJ -- required for top-10 on a summit finish
- **Aerobic gold standard**: 4 W/kg for 4 hrs with <5% HR drift
- **Winter volume**: ~300 hrs Nov 1 - Feb 28 for riders graduating to WT
- **Key limiter**: Fatigue resistance -- ability to access power over threshold past 2500 kJ of work

[Source: "The Making of a Pro Tour Rider" -- Nate Wilson, TrainingPeaks]

| Hilly stage | 3500-4500 | 700-850 | 3500-4500 |
| Mountain stage | 4000-5500 | 750-900 | 4000-5500 |

### Key Tactical Insights

1. **Breakaway energy cost**: Riders in a break expend 0.8-1.9 W/kg more than the peloton per hour. Drafting saves 30-40% of energy.

2. **Mountain stage pattern**: Peak short-duration powers (1-5 min) are usually set in the opening kilometers when the break goes or on the first climb. Peak sustained powers (12-20 min) are set on the final 1-2 decisive climbs.

3. **Race progression**: NP in the first half of stages is often dramatically lower than the second half (e.g., Rogers Stage 7: 231 W vs 316 W). Flat stages see peak power in final 30-60 min.

4. **Altitude penalty**: 8-12% FTP reduction at 1500-2600 m. Riders acclimatized to altitude show less degradation.

5. **Stage-to-stage fatigue**: After 3 weeks, ITT performance of ~5.5 W/kg for 40 min is described as "something few people can achieve." Daily TSS ceiling appears to be ~266 regardless of race format.

6. **Cobbles**: Not about sustained power but about repeated micro-surges (600-1200 W) with zero recovery between sectors. Physiologically distinct from normal road racing.

7. **GC threshold**: 6.0 W/kg on a 15-20 min decisive climb = "the magic number" for GC contention at the Tour de France.
