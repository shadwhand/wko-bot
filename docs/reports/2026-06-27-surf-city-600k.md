- **Ice socks/vest** at King City control (km 360) — 10-15 min of core cooling

- Start 10-14 days before the event
- 60-90 min of moderate intensity in heat per day
- Sauna protocol if outdoor heat training not available: 20-30 min post-ride

## Autoregulation & Race-Day Adaptation (from TMT-74/75)

### Undercook, Don't Overcook

- **"Undercook by 5-20% beats overcook by 10%"** -- when debating between pushing and holding back on a 600k, always hold back [E] (TMT-75). This applies everywhere: the coastal section at night, the La Honda climbs, and especially the Salinas Valley heat. If in doubt, ride easier.

### Terrain-Specific FTP Variance

- **Expect 10-20W FTP variance between terrain types** -- climbing FTP is often lower than flat FTP, and this is normal, not a sign of fading [E] (TMT-74). On the La Honda/Haskins section (km 94-152), do NOT panic if power is 10-15W below what you held on the flat coast. Adjust targets per-terrain rather than chasing a single number.

### Trust Feel Over Power After Hour 15

- **FTP by-feel: experienced athletes get within ~5W in 10 min** -- after 15+ hours, trust perceived exertion over the power meter [E] (TMT-74). Cardiac drift, fatigue, and temperature all decouple watts from internal cost. The power number that felt sustainable at hour 3 may be overcooking at hour 20.
- **RPE should override power targets after hour 15** -- "breathing > power" [E] (TMT-75). If breathing feels labored at your target watts, drop watts. The body integrates signals the power meter cannot see.

### Autoregulate the Plan

- **100% plan compliance is a warning sign, not a badge of honor** [E] (TMT-75). If you are hitting every target exactly, you are probably ignoring signals that should be causing adjustments. The pacing table above is a guide, not a contract. Autoregulate based on how you feel, what the temperature is doing, and how the gut is behaving.

### Root Cause: Hypothermia from Insufficient Clothing → Cascade Failure

The fitness and fueling were excellent. This was purely an equipment/thermoregulation failure.

**Chain of events (from athlete's Strava notes):**

1. **Perfect ride through 400km.** Nutrition 70-80g/hr, sub-24hr pace, food plan executed exactly (poke bowl in Marina, breakfast burrito in Pescadero). No fueling issues.
2. **After ~250mi refill stop, stepped outside into cold.** Only had summer-weight layers: long sleeve jersey + wind-blocking jersey. **No down jacket (left it), no LS gloves (forgotten at home), no tights (decided against for weight).**
3. **Monterey Bay headwind added wind chill.** Core temp started dropping. Shivering anytime pedaling stopped.
4. **Hypothermia → nausea** (cold-induced, NOT glycogen-related). Progressively worse as core temp dropped further.
5. **Nausea → couldn't eat** → NOW glycogen becomes a secondary issue. The spiral begins.
6. **Falling asleep on bike** → 15min ditch nap became 30min → got colder, couldn't rewarm.
7. **Wrapped short-sleeve jersey around hands** for improvised gloves. Still couldn't maintain body temp.
8. **DNF at 499km.** Realized it would only get colder and foggier toward the coast. Called Uber.

**The athlete had the sub-24hr in the bag.** Headwind died as predicted on turnaround. This was a preventable equipment failure, not a fitness or nutrition failure.

### 2024 → 2026 Fixes

| 2024 Problem | What Happened | 2026 Fix |
|---|---|---|
| No insulating layers | Left down jacket, forgot LS gloves, skipped tights for weight | **Mandatory night kit: Ornot Alpha vest, thermal bibs/tights, warm gloves, beanie/neck gaiter.** Weighs <500g, saves the ride. |
| Tried to ride through cold | Chasing sub-24hr, ignored thermoregulation warning signs | **Stop and warm up. 30-40 min of coffee/soup is cheaper than a DNF.** Sacrifice clock time, not the finish. |
| No plan for 25°C temp swing | Monterey Bay night fog + headwind at 12°C in summer kit | **Layer up BEFORE you feel cold.** At King City (km 425), full night kit goes on. Non-negotiable. |
| Cold-induced nausea → couldn't eat | Hypothermia caused nausea, not the other way around | **Warm first, eat second.** If nauseous from cold: stop, put on every layer, hot drink, wait 15 min. THEN eat. |
| Started slightly fast | 172W first 50km (IF ~0.70) | **Cap first 100km at IF 0.60.** You proved the fitness — now protect it. |

**The difference between DNF and finish is 500g of warm clothing. The fitness was there. The fueling was there. The plan was there. Pack the damn jacket.**
